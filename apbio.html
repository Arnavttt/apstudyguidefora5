Enzymes

Enzyme Function

Biological catalysts ŌĆö lower activation energy. Not consumed. Substrate binds active site (induced fit model). Highly specific (shape complementarity).

Factors Affecting Rate

Temperature: increases rate up to optimum, then denatures. pH: optimum varies (pepsin pH 2, trypsin pH 8). Substrate concentration: rate increases until saturation (Vmax).

Inhibition

Competitive: blocks active site, reversible with Ōåæ substrate. Noncompetitive: binds allosteric site, changes shape, cannot overcome with Ōåæ substrate. Feedback inhibition = end product inhibits pathway enzyme.

Evolution & Natural Selection

Mechanisms of Evolution

Natural Selection

Heritable variation + differential reproductive success = evolution. Directional (shifts mean), stabilizing (eliminates extremes), disruptive (favors both extremes).

Genetic Drift

Random allele frequency change ŌĆö strongest in small populations. Bottleneck effect (disaster reduces population). Founder effect (small group colonizes new area).

Hardy-Weinberg Equilibrium

p┬▓ + 2pq + q┬▓ = 1; p + q = 1. No evolution when: large population, random mating, no mutation/migration/selection. Useful for detecting evolution.

Speciation

Allopatric: geographic barrier separates populations. Sympatric: within same area (polyploidy in plants, habitat differentiation). Reproductive isolation = true species.

Exponential growth: dN/dt = rN (unlimited resources). Logistic growth: dN/dt = rN(KŌłÆN)/K. K = carrying capacity. r-selected (many offspring, short life) vs. K-selected (few offspring, long life).

Predation (+/ŌłÆ), competition (ŌłÆ/ŌłÆ), mutualism (+/+), commensalism (+/0), parasitism (+/ŌłÆ). Character displacement: competing species diverge in sympatry. Keystone species: disproportionate ecosystem effect.

Ecosystem & Energy

~10% energy transfer between trophic levels. Gross productivity vs. net productivity. Decomposers return nutrients. Biogeochemical cycles: carbon, nitrogen, phosphorus, water.

Succession

Primary: bare rock ŌåÆ pioneer species (lichen) ŌåÆ climax community. Secondary: after disturbance, soil present. Biodiversity generally increases through succession.
