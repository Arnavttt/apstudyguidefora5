Unit 1 · ~6–8% of exam
Living World: Ecosystems

Energy Flow & Nutrient Cycles

Energy Pyramid

~10% energy transfers between trophic levels. Producers → Primary consumers → Secondary → Tertiary. Energy lost as heat (2nd law of thermodynamics). Explains why top predators are rare and why vegetarian diets are more energy-efficient.

Carbon Cycle

Photosynthesis fixes CO₂. Respiration + decomposition releases it. Fossil fuel burning releases stored carbon. Ocean absorbs ~30% of CO₂ (acidification risk). Deforestation reduces carbon sinks.

Nitrogen Cycle

N₂ unusable by most organisms. Nitrogen fixation (bacteria, lightning) → NH₃. Nitrification → NO₃⁻ (usable). Denitrification → N₂. Key: Rhizobium in legume root nodules fix N₂. Fertilizer runoff → eutrophication.

Biomes

Determined by temperature and precipitation. Tropical rainforest (high both) → tundra (low both). Temperate deciduous forest, grassland, desert, taiga/boreal, chaparral. Ocean biomes: coral reef (most productive), open ocean, estuary (most productive per area).

Unit 2 · ~6–8% of exam
Biodiversity

Threats to Biodiversity (HIPPCO)

HIPPCO — Biodiversity Threats (in order of impact)
  • H — Habitat destruction: #1 threat — deforestation, wetland drainage, urban sprawl
  • I — Invasive species: outcompete natives with no natural predators (kudzu, cane toad, zebra mussel)
  • P — Population growth: increased resource demand, habitat pressure
  • P — Pollution: DDT (bald eagle thinning), eutrophication, ocean plastics
  • C — Climate change: shifting habitats, coral bleaching, phenological mismatch
  • O — Overexploitation: overhunting, overfishing, bushmeat trade

Conservation Strategies

Protected Areas

National parks, wildlife refuges, marine reserves. Edge effects (disturbed habitat at borders). Habitat corridors connect fragments. Larger reserves generally better (species-area relationship).

Ecosystem Services

Provisioning (food, water, timber). Regulating (climate, flood control, pollination). Cultural (recreation, aesthetics). Supporting (nutrient cycling, soil formation). Valuing services = economic argument for conservation.

Populations

Population Growth & Calculations

EXPONENTIAL GROWTH: dN/dt = rN (J-curve)
LOGISTIC GROWTH: dN/dt = rN(K−N)/K (S-curve)
K = carrying capacity. Growth rate max when N = K/2.

RULE OF 70: doubling time (years) = 70 / growth rate (%)
Example: 2% growth → doubles in 35 years

TOTAL FERTILITY RATE (TFR):
Replacement level ≈ 2.1 children/woman.
TFR > 2.1 → population grows. Below → shrinks.

Unit 9 · ~15–20% of exam
Global Change

Climate Change — The Science

Greenhouse Effect

GHGs (CO₂, CH₄, N₂O, H₂O, O₃) absorb infrared radiation re-emitted by Earth and re-radiate it back. CO₂ has risen from ~280 ppm (pre-industrial) to 420+ ppm. Enhanced GE → global warming.

Consequences

Sea level rise (thermal expansion + glacial melt). More intense storms, droughts, floods. Ocean acidification (CO₂ + H₂O → H₂CO₃). Coral bleaching. Species range shifts. Arctic amplification (albedo feedback).

Ozone Depletion

CFCs break down stratospheric ozone (O₃). O₃ absorbs UV-B radiation (causes cancer, cataracts, ecosystem harm). Montreal Protocol (1987) phased out CFCs — ozone layer recovering. DIFFERENT from climate change!

Solutions

Mitigation: reduce GHGs (renewables, efficiency, carbon pricing, reforestation). Adaptation: sea walls, drought-resistant crops, early warning systems. Carbon capture. International agreements (Paris Agreement, though enforcement is weak).

⚡ Perfect Score Checklist

  • 10% energy rule: only 10% of energy transfers between trophic levels
  • HIPPCO: Habitat destruction is #1 threat to biodiversity
  • Rule of 70: doubling time = 70 ÷ growth rate
  • Replacement TFR ≈ 2.1; below = population decline
  • Eutrophication = excess N/P → algae bloom → oxygen depletion → dead zone
  • Ozone ≠ climate change: ozone = CFCs + UV; climate = CO₂ + IR radiation
  • Montreal Protocol saved ozone; Paris Agreement targets climate
  • APES FRQ always includes a calculation — show all work with units
  • Coal = most CO₂ per unit energy; nuclear = no GHG during operation
  • Keystone species have disproportionate ecosystem impact relative to their biomass
