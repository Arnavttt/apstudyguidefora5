Research Methods

Correlation vs. Causation

Correlation shows relationship between variables (−1 to +1). Does NOT prove causation. Confounding variables explain many correlations. Only experiment can establish causation.

Experimental Method

Independent variable (manipulated) → Dependent variable (measured). Control group vs. experimental group. Random assignment controls confounds. Double-blind: neither subjects nor researchers know condition.

Key Research Concepts

Reliability: consistent results. Validity: measures what it claims to. Operational definition: precise definition of variables. Sampling bias: non-representative sample → cannot generalize.

Ethics in Research

IRB approval, informed consent, debriefing, right to withdraw. Animal research: minimize suffering. Milgram and Zimbardo studies raised major ethical concerns → modern standards.

Unit 2 · ~8–10% of exam
Biological Bases of Behavior

Neurons & Neurotransmitters

Neuron Structure

Dendrites (receive) → Cell body → Axon → Terminal buttons → Synapse. Action potential: all-or-nothing. Myelin sheath speeds transmission. Refractory period after firing.

Key Neurotransmitters

Dopamine: reward, movement (low → Parkinson's; excess → schizophrenia). Serotonin: mood, sleep (low → depression). Norepinephrine: alertness, arousal. GABA: inhibitory, calming. Acetylcholine: memory, muscle movement. Endorphins: pain relief, pleasure.

Brain Regions

Limbic system: amygdala (fear/emotion), hippocampus (memory formation). Frontal lobe: planning, judgment, personality. Parietal: spatial processing, touch. Occipital: vision. Temporal: hearing, language. Broca's area: speech production. Wernicke's: language comprehension.

Nervous System

CNS (brain + spinal cord) vs. PNS. Somatic (voluntary) vs. Autonomic. Sympathetic (fight-or-flight: ↑HR, ↑breathing) vs. Parasympathetic (rest-and-digest: ↓HR, restores). Reflex arcs bypass brain.

Unit 4 · ~7–10% of exam
Learning

Classical Conditioning (Pavlov)

Classical Conditioning Terms
  • UCS (Unconditioned Stimulus): naturally causes response (food)
  • UCR (Unconditioned Response): natural response (salivation)
  • CS (Conditioned Stimulus): neutral stimulus paired with UCS (bell)
  • CR (Conditioned Response): learned response to CS (salivation to bell)
  • Extinction: CR weakens when CS presented without UCS
  • Spontaneous Recovery: extinguished CR reappears after rest period
  • Generalization: responding to stimuli similar to CS
  • Discrimination: responding only to specific CS

Operant Conditioning (Skinner)

TypeStimulusEffect on BehaviorExample
Positive ReinforcementAdd pleasant stimulusIncreases behaviorGive candy for good behavior
Negative ReinforcementRemove unpleasant stimulusIncreases behaviorTurn off alarm when you wake up
Positive PunishmentAdd unpleasant stimulusDecreases behaviorSpeeding ticket

Reinforcement Schedules

Fixed ratio (FR): after set # responses — high, steady rate. Variable ratio (VR): unpredictable # — highest rate, most resistant to extinction (slot machines). Fixed interval (FI): scallop pattern. Variable interval (VI): steady, slow rate.

Observational Learning (Bandura)

Bobo Doll experiment. Modeling — learning by watching others. Vicarious reinforcement. Requires: attention, retention, reproduction, motivation. Media violence debate.

Unit 5 · ~13–17% of exam
Cognition, Memory & Language

Memory Model

Sensory Memory

Brief, high capacity. Iconic (visual, ~0.5s), Echoic (auditory, ~3–4s). Most info lost immediately; attended info moves to STM.

Short-Term / Working Memory

7±2 chunks (Miller). ~20–30 seconds without rehearsal. Maintenance rehearsal keeps info. Elaborative rehearsal → LTM. Working memory = active manipulation (Baddeley).

Long-Term Memory

Explicit/Declarative: episodic (personal events) + semantic (facts). Implicit: procedural (skills), classical conditioning. Hippocampus = explicit memory. Cerebellum = procedural.

Memory Failures

Encoding failure (never stored). Decay. Interference: proactive (old blocks new), retroactive (new blocks old). Source amnesia. Misinformation effect (Loftus). Anterograde/retrograde amnesia.

Unit 9 · ~12–14% of exam
Psychological Disorders & Treatment

Major Disorders

Mood Disorders

Major Depression: sad mood, loss of interest, 2+ weeks. Biopsychosocial model — serotonin, norepinephrine. Bipolar: manic + depressive episodes. Mania: elevated mood, grandiosity, reduced sleep. Treated with lithium.

Anxiety Disorders

GAD (pervasive worry). Panic disorder. Phobias (specific, social). OCD (obsessions + compulsions, now separate category). PTSD (trauma-related). All involve exaggerated fear response.

Schizophrenia

Positive symptoms (add): hallucinations, delusions, disorganized speech. Negative symptoms (subtract): flat affect, poverty of speech, avolition. Dopamine hypothesis (excess). Antipsychotics block dopamine receptors.

Personality Disorders

Cluster A (odd): paranoid, schizoid. Cluster B (dramatic): antisocial (no empathy, manipulative), borderline (unstable relationships, identity, mood), narcissistic, histrionic. Cluster C (anxious): avoidant, dependent, OCD.

Q1Operant Conditioning

A parent stops nagging a child every time the child cleans their room. The child cleans their room more often as a result. This is an example of:

  • A) Positive reinforcement
  • B) Negative reinforcement
  • C) Positive punishment
  • D) Negative punishment
Answer: B — Negative reinforcement = removing an aversive stimulus (nagging) to INCREASE a behavior (cleaning). The "negative" refers to removal, not something bad. Both types of reinforcement increase behavior.

⚡ Perfect Score Checklist

  • Negative reinforcement INCREASES behavior (removing aversive stimulus) — most common confusion on exam
  • Correlation ≠ causation — only experiment establishes cause
  • Independent variable = manipulated; dependent variable = measured
  • Dopamine = reward + movement; Serotonin = mood; GABA = inhibitory; Acetylcholine = memory
  • Hippocampus = memory formation (not storage); amygdala = fear/emotion
  • Variable ratio schedule = most resistant to extinction (gambling/slot machines)
  • Bystander effect: more witnesses = less helping (diffusion of responsibility)
  • Fundamental Attribution Error: overestimate disposition, underestimate situation for OTHERS
  • CBT is the most evidence-based treatment for most anxiety and mood disorders
  • Anterograde amnesia = cannot form new memories (hippocampus damage)
