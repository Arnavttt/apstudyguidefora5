Q1
Formal analysis in art history involves
  • A) researching the historical context and patronage only
  • B) identifying the artist's biography
  • C) listing the date and medium
  • D) describing and analyzing the visual elements (line, shape, color, space, texture, composition) and principles of design
D is correct. describing and analyzing the visual elements (line, shape, color, space, texture, composition) and principles of design
Q2
Iconography in art history is the study of
  • A) subject matter, symbols, and the meaning of imagery in works of art
  • B) the physical materials used to create a work
  • C) the stylistic evolution of an artist's career
  • D) the provenance and ownership history of works
A is correct. subject matter, symbols, and the meaning of imagery in works of art
Q3
The Venus of Willendorf (c. 28,000–25,000 BCE) is significant because it
  • A) was created using advanced metalworking techniques
  • B) depicts a named historical figure
  • C) is one of the earliest known examples of representational art, possibly associated with fertility or female identity
  • D) is the first painted image in human history
C is correct. is one of the earliest known examples of representational art, possibly associated with fertility or female identity
Q4
The Great Pyramids at Giza (c. 2550–2490 BCE) functioned as
  • A) administrative centers for the Egyptian empire
  • B) royal tombs for pharaohs, designed to facilitate the king's journey to the afterlife and demonstrate royal power
  • C) temples for public religious worship
  • D) astronomical observatories
B is correct. royal tombs for pharaohs, designed to facilitate the king's journey to the afterlife and demonstrate royal power
Q5
The Parthenon (Athens, 447–432 BCE) exemplifies the Classical Greek ideal of
  • A) Hellenistic emotional expressiveness
  • B) Byzantine spiritual abstraction
  • C) harmonia (harmony), proportion, and idealized human form through its mathematical ratios and sculptural program
  • D) raw naturalism and individual portraiture
C is correct. harmonia (harmony), proportion, and idealized human form through its mathematical ratios and sculptural program
Q6
Roman art and architecture differed from Greek by emphasizing
  • A) realistic portraiture, narrative historical relief, and engineering innovations (arch, vault, concrete)
  • B) idealized human perfection and mythological subjects only
  • C) abstract non-representational decoration
  • D) small-scale portable objects exclusively
A is correct. realistic portraiture, narrative historical relief, and engineering innovations (arch, vault, concrete)
Q7
The term "linear perspective" (developed in the Renaissance) refers to
  • A) the use of overlapping figures to suggest depth
  • B) a system of symbolic colors representing spiritual hierarchy
  • C) painting figures smaller in the foreground
  • D) a mathematical system of depicting three-dimensional space on a flat surface using converging lines meeting at a vanishing point
D is correct. a mathematical system of depicting three-dimensional space on a flat surface using converging lines meeting at a vanishing point
Q8
The Hagia Sophia (Istanbul, 532–537 CE) is architecturally significant for
  • A) its pointed Gothic arches and flying buttresses
  • B) its strict adherence to Greek temple proportions
  • C) being the first church built in the Roman Empire
  • D) its massive dome appearing to float on a ring of windows, creating an ethereal interior space for Byzantine worship
D is correct. its massive dome appearing to float on a ring of windows, creating an ethereal interior space for Byzantine worship
Q9
Ukiyo-e prints in Japanese art (Edo period) depicted
  • A) battle scenes commissioned by samurai patrons
  • B) the "floating world" — subjects like kabuki actors, courtesans, and landscapes, produced as affordable woodblock prints
  • C) imperial court ceremonies and royal portraits exclusively
  • D) religious Buddhist imagery for temple use
B is correct. the "floating world" — subjects like kabuki actors, courtesans, and landscapes, produced as affordable woodblock prints
Q10
The development of the Gothic cathedral in medieval Europe was enabled by
  • A) the ribbed vault and flying buttress, which redirected structural forces and allowed thinner walls with large stained glass windows
  • B) Roman concrete construction techniques
  • C) Islamic tilework and decorative patterns
  • D) Byzantine mosaic programs
A is correct. the ribbed vault and flying buttress, which redirected structural forces and allowed thinner walls with large stained glass windows
Q11
African art is often "functional" in the sense that
  • A) African art has no aesthetic dimension
  • B) all African objects are made for sale to outsiders
  • C) African art is always abstract with no recognizable imagery
  • D) many objects serve ritual, ceremonial, or social purposes (masks, figures, thrones) rather than being purely aesthetic
D is correct. many objects serve ritual, ceremonial, or social purposes (masks, figures, thrones) rather than being purely aesthetic
Q12
The Aztec Sun Stone (Coyolxauhqui Stone, Tenochtitlan, c. 1500 CE) represents
  • A) a cosmological and calendrical monument depicting Aztec creation myth and timekeeping
  • B) a legal code inscribed in stone
  • C) a map of the Aztec Empire
  • D) a portrait of the ruling emperor
A is correct. a cosmological and calendrical monument depicting Aztec creation myth and timekeeping
Q13
The "hierarchy of scale" in ancient and medieval art refers to
  • A) depicting more important figures larger than less important ones, regardless of physical distance or spatial logic
  • B) using perspective to make distant figures smaller
  • C) showing divine beings at the top of the composition always
  • D) a rule that all figures must be the same size
A is correct. depicting more important figures larger than less important ones, regardless of physical distance or spatial logic
Q14
Impressionist painters (e.g., Monet, Renoir, 1860s–1880s France) were revolutionary for
  • A) depicting only mythological and historical subjects
  • B) reviving medieval gold-leaf techniques
  • C) capturing momentary light, atmosphere, and everyday scenes with loose, visible brushwork rather than polished academic finish
  • D) using geometric abstraction to eliminate all realistic representation
C is correct. capturing momentary light, atmosphere, and everyday scenes with loose, visible brushwork rather than polished academic finish
Q15
The term "avant-garde" in early 20th-century art history refers to
  • A) art critics who evaluated exhibitions
  • B) patrons who funded new art movements
  • C) artists who pushed boundaries ahead of mainstream taste, experimenting with new styles (Cubism, Fauvism, Dada, Surrealism)
  • D) conservative artists defending academic traditions
C is correct. artists who pushed boundaries ahead of mainstream taste, experimenting with new styles (Cubism, Fauvism, Dada, Surrealism)
Q16
In Islamic art and architecture, figural representation is often avoided in religious contexts in favor of
  • A) portrait sculpture of religious leaders
  • B) landscape painting of sacred sites
  • C) geometric patterns, arabesque (floral interlace), and calligraphy (the word of God)
  • D) narrative fresco cycles depicting prophet stories
C is correct. geometric patterns, arabesque (floral interlace), and calligraphy (the word of God)
Q17
The Baroque style (17th century) is characterized by
  • A) dramatic use of light and shadow (chiaroscuro), emotional intensity, movement, and theatrical compositions
  • B) geometric simplicity and flat, even lighting
  • C) strict adherence to classical Greek proportions
  • D) abstract rejection of all figurative imagery
A is correct. dramatic use of light and shadow (chiaroscuro), emotional intensity, movement, and theatrical compositions
Q18
Frida Kahlo's self-portraits are important in 20th-century art history because they
  • A) introduced photography as fine art in Mexico
  • B) rejected all political content in favor of pure form
  • C) explore personal identity, bodily trauma, Mexican cultural heritage, and gender through a Surrealist-influenced style
  • D) established the principles of American Abstract Expressionism
C is correct. explore personal identity, bodily trauma, Mexican cultural heritage, and gender through a Surrealist-influenced style
Q19
The readymade, pioneered by Marcel Duchamp (e.g., "Fountain," 1917), challenged art history by
  • A) reviving ancient classical sculpture traditions
  • B) proposing that any ordinary object becomes art when designated as such by the artist, questioning authorship and aesthetics
  • C) showing that industrial manufacturing could produce beautiful objects
  • D) celebrating craft and skilled technique
B is correct. proposing that any ordinary object becomes art when designated as such by the artist, questioning authorship and aesthetics
Q20
Postmodern art (1970s–present) is distinguished from modernism by
  • A) its embrace of appropriation, irony, pastiche, and questioning of the "grand narratives" of progress and originality
  • B) strict adherence to pure abstraction without reference to mass culture
  • C) rejecting all technology and industrial materials
  • D) return to strict figurative realism
A is correct. its embrace of appropriation, irony, pastiche, and questioning of the "grand narratives" of progress and originality
