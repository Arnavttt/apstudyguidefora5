Q1
The fluid mosaic model describes the cell membrane as
  • A) a double-stranded DNA molecule
  • B) a phospholipid bilayer with embedded proteins that move laterally
  • C) a rigid protein scaffold surrounding the cell
  • D) a solid lipid layer with fixed channels
B is correct. a phospholipid bilayer with embedded proteins that move laterally
Q2
During cellular respiration, the electron transport chain produces most ATP via
  • A) the citric acid cycle directly
  • B) fermentation in the absence of oxygen
  • C) chemiosmosis — proton flow through ATP synthase driven by an electrochemical gradient
  • D) substrate-level phosphorylation in glycolysis
C is correct. chemiosmosis — proton flow through ATP synthase driven by an electrochemical gradient
Q3
In photosynthesis, the light-dependent reactions occur in the
  • A) cytoplasm of the cell
  • B) outer chloroplast membrane
  • C) thylakoid membranes, where light energy splits water and produces ATP, NADPH, and O₂
  • D) stroma, where CO₂ is fixed
C is correct. thylakoid membranes, where light energy splits water and produces ATP, NADPH, and O₂
Q4
A gene is best defined as
  • A) a segment of DNA that encodes a functional product (protein or RNA)
  • B) any sequence of nucleotides in the genome
  • C) a sequence of amino acids
  • D) the entire chromosome
A is correct. a segment of DNA that encodes a functional product (protein or RNA)
Q5
Mendel's law of independent assortment states that
  • A) alleles of different genes are distributed to gametes independently of one another (for genes on different chromosomes)
  • B) alleles of the same gene always stay together
  • C) dominant alleles mask recessive ones
  • D) each parent contributes equal traits to offspring
A is correct. alleles of different genes are distributed to gametes independently of one another (for genes on different chromosomes)
Q6
Natural selection acts on
  • A) phenotype (observable traits), but heritable genetic variation is what is actually selected
  • B) genotype directly, altering DNA sequences
  • C) the species as a whole, not individuals
  • D) mutations before they are expressed
A is correct. phenotype (observable traits), but heritable genetic variation is what is actually selected
Q7
The Hardy-Weinberg principle states that allele frequencies in a population remain constant if
  • A) the environment is stable over time
  • B) there is no genetic variation within the population
  • C) the population is large, mating is random, and there is no mutation, migration, or selection
  • D) all individuals survive and reproduce equally regardless
C is correct. the population is large, mating is random, and there is no mutation, migration, or selection
Q8
DNA replication is described as semi-conservative because
  • A) both strands are completely new in each daughter molecule
  • B) only one daughter molecule retains original DNA
  • C) the process requires one round of RNA synthesis
  • D) each new double helix consists of one original (template) strand and one newly synthesized strand
D is correct. each new double helix consists of one original (template) strand and one newly synthesized strand
Q9
The role of mRNA in protein synthesis is to
  • A) splice introns from pre-mRNA
  • B) carry the genetic code from the nucleus to the ribosome as a template for translation
  • C) catalyze peptide bond formation
  • D) carry amino acids to the ribosome
B is correct. carry the genetic code from the nucleus to the ribosome as a template for translation
Q10
A frameshift mutation results from
  • A) substituting one base pair for another
  • B) inverting a segment of DNA
  • C) duplicating an entire chromosome
  • D) the insertion or deletion of nucleotides not in multiples of three, shifting the reading frame
D is correct. the insertion or deletion of nucleotides not in multiples of three, shifting the reading frame
Q11
Osmoregulation in freshwater fish involves
  • A) producing concentrated urine and drinking saltwater
  • B) allowing osmosis to equilibrate internal and external water
  • C) relying on food intake for all ion balance
  • D) continuously excreting large amounts of dilute urine and actively absorbing ions across gills
D is correct. continuously excreting large amounts of dilute urine and actively absorbing ions across gills
Q12
Action potentials are propagated along an axon by
  • A) sequential opening of voltage-gated Na⁺ channels, depolarizing adjacent membrane regions
  • B) ATP-powered pumps pushing ions along the axon
  • C) calcium influx at the synapse
  • D) myelin sheath shortening the axon length
A is correct. sequential opening of voltage-gated Na⁺ channels, depolarizing adjacent membrane regions
Q13
Which process directly introduces new alleles into a population?
  • A) Genetic drift
  • B) Gene flow
  • C) Mutation
  • D) Natural selection
C is correct. Mutation
Q14
The role of decomposers in an ecosystem is to
  • A) transfer energy from producers to consumers
  • B) break down dead organic matter, releasing nutrients back into the environment
  • C) fix nitrogen from the atmosphere
  • D) convert inorganic molecules to glucose
B is correct. break down dead organic matter, releasing nutrients back into the environment
Q15
Convergent evolution produces
  • A) rapid speciation through reproductive isolation
  • B) similar traits in unrelated species due to similar selective pressures
  • C) identical genotypes in geographically isolated populations
  • D) common ancestry shared between two species
B is correct. similar traits in unrelated species due to similar selective pressures
Q16
The Calvin cycle (light-independent reactions) uses __________ to fix CO₂ into sugar.
  • A) FADH₂ and NADH from the electron transport chain
  • B) glucose directly from glycolysis
  • C) oxygen released from water splitting
  • D) ATP and NADPH produced by the light-dependent reactions
D is correct. ATP and NADPH produced by the light-dependent reactions
Q17
Enzyme inhibitors that bind at the active site and compete with the substrate are called
  • A) allosteric activators
  • B) coenzymes
  • C) competitive inhibitors
  • D) noncompetitive inhibitors
C is correct. competitive inhibitors
Q18
A clonal selection explains how
  • A) the body produces random antibodies after every infection
  • B) vaccines introduce live pathogens to stimulate immunity
  • C) antigen binding activates specific B cells, which proliferate and differentiate into antibody-secreting plasma cells
  • D) T cells destroy all foreign cells equally
C is correct. antigen binding activates specific B cells, which proliferate and differentiate into antibody-secreting plasma cells
Q19
In mitosis, sister chromatids separate during
  • A) anaphase
  • B) metaphase
  • C) prophase
  • D) telophase
A is correct. anaphase
Q20
The endosymbiotic theory proposes that
  • A) eukaryotes evolved directly from viruses
  • B) all organelles formed by infolding of the plasma membrane
  • C) bacteria gained organelles by horizontal gene transfer
  • D) mitochondria and chloroplasts evolved from free-living prokaryotes engulfed by ancestral eukaryotic cells
D is correct. mitochondria and chloroplasts evolved from free-living prokaryotes engulfed by ancestral eukaryotic cells
