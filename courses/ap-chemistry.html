Q1
Electronegativity generally increases across a period because
  • A) nuclear charge increases while atomic radius decreases, pulling bonding electrons more strongly
  • B) more electrons shield the nucleus
  • C) fewer protons are present
  • D) electron affinity decreases
A is correct. nuclear charge increases while atomic radius decreases, pulling bonding electrons more strongly
Q2
A polar covalent bond forms when
  • A) two nonmetal atoms share electrons unequally due to a difference in electronegativity
  • B) electrons are fully transferred from one atom to another
  • C) two identical atoms share electrons equally
  • D) a metal bonds with a nonmetal
A is correct. two nonmetal atoms share electrons unequally due to a difference in electronegativity
Q3
According to VSEPR theory, a molecule with 4 bonding pairs and 0 lone pairs on the central atom has
  • A) linear geometry
  • B) a tetrahedral geometry
  • C) trigonal planar geometry
  • D) square planar geometry
B is correct. a tetrahedral geometry
Q4
The ideal gas law (PV = nRT) assumes that gas molecules
  • A) have no volume and experience no intermolecular forces
  • B) have significant volume and strong attractions
  • C) travel in curved paths due to gravity
  • D) exchange energy unequally in collisions
A is correct. have no volume and experience no intermolecular forces
Q5
Le Chatelier's principle predicts that adding a product to an equilibrium mixture will
  • A) have no effect if temperature is constant
  • B) increase the rate constant for the forward reaction
  • C) shift the equilibrium toward the reactants (to reduce the added product)
  • D) shift the equilibrium toward more products
C is correct. shift the equilibrium toward the reactants (to reduce the added product)
Q6
The equilibrium constant Kc for a reaction is
  • A) the ratio of forward to reverse reaction rates at any time
  • B) a measure of how fast a reaction proceeds
  • C) equal to the reaction quotient at all times
  • D) the ratio of product concentrations to reactant concentrations, each raised to their stoichiometric coefficients
D is correct. the ratio of product concentrations to reactant concentrations, each raised to their stoichiometric coefficients
Q7
A buffer resists pH changes because it contains
  • A) a strong acid and strong base in equal amounts
  • B) excess hydroxide ions
  • C) a salt that hydrolyzes water
  • D) a weak acid and its conjugate base in significant amounts
D is correct. a weak acid and its conjugate base in significant amounts
Q8
Hess's Law states that
  • A) bond energies determine reaction rate
  • B) the enthalpy change for a reaction equals the sum of enthalpy changes of any series of steps that produce the same overall reaction
  • C) exothermic reactions always have negative entropy changes
  • D) enthalpy changes depend on the path taken
B is correct. the enthalpy change for a reaction equals the sum of enthalpy changes of any series of steps that produce the same overall reaction
Q9
The rate-determining step of a mechanism is the
  • A) slowest step, which controls the overall rate of the reaction
  • B) step that involves the most molecules
  • C) first step of the mechanism always
  • D) step with the lowest activation energy
A is correct. slowest step, which controls the overall rate of the reaction
Q10
A catalyst increases reaction rate by
  • A) raising the concentrations of reactants
  • B) shifting the equilibrium toward products
  • C) providing an alternative reaction pathway with a lower activation energy
  • D) increasing the temperature of the reaction
C is correct. providing an alternative reaction pathway with a lower activation energy
Q11
Oxidation is defined as
  • A) the loss of protons
  • B) the loss of electrons (increase in oxidation number)
  • C) the gain of electrons
  • D) the gain of oxygen atoms only
B is correct. the loss of electrons (increase in oxidation number)
Q12
In a galvanic (voltaic) cell, oxidation occurs at the
  • A) positive electrode
  • B) anode
  • C) cathode
  • D) salt bridge
B is correct. anode
Q13
The pH of a 0.01 M HCl solution (strong acid) is
  • A) 2
  • B) 12
  • C) 4
  • D) 7
A is correct. 2
Q14
Entropy (S) measures
  • A) the heat content of a system
  • B) the free energy available for work
  • C) the bond energy of a molecule
  • D) the degree of disorder or number of microstates available to a system
D is correct. the degree of disorder or number of microstates available to a system
Q15
A spontaneous reaction at all temperatures must have
  • A) both ΔH and ΔS negative
  • B) negative ΔH and positive ΔS (ΔG = ΔH − TΔS < 0 for all T)
  • C) positive ΔH and negative ΔS
  • D) ΔG = 0 at all temperatures
B is correct. negative ΔH and positive ΔS (ΔG = ΔH − TΔS < 0 for all T)
Q16
Intermolecular forces from weakest to strongest are
  • A) ionic → hydrogen bonding → London dispersion
  • B) covalent bonds → dipole–dipole → London dispersion
  • C) London dispersion → dipole–dipole → hydrogen bonding
  • D) hydrogen bonding → dipole–dipole → London dispersion
C is correct. London dispersion → dipole–dipole → hydrogen bonding
Q17
Beer-Lambert Law (A = εlc) relates absorbance to
  • A) molar absorptivity, path length, and concentration of the absorbing species
  • B) wavelength, frequency, and photon energy
  • C) temperature, pressure, and volume
  • D) reaction rate and activation energy
A is correct. molar absorptivity, path length, and concentration of the absorbing species
Q18
For a reaction 2A → B, the rate law is rate = k[A]². If [A] is doubled, the rate
  • A) remains the same
  • B) quadruples (increases by a factor of 4)
  • C) doubles
  • D) increases 8-fold
B is correct. quadruples (increases by a factor of 4)
Q19
Lattice energy is the energy required to
  • A) dissolve an ionic compound in water
  • B) form one mole of ionic bonds from neutral atoms
  • C) vaporize a metallic solid
  • D) separate an ionic solid into its gaseous ions completely
D is correct. separate an ionic solid into its gaseous ions completely
Q20
Colligative properties of a solution depend on
  • A) the number of dissolved particles (solute concentration), not the identity of the solute
  • B) the molecular weight of the solute
  • C) the polarity of the solvent
  • D) the temperature of the solution only
A is correct. the number of dissolved particles (solute concentration), not the identity of the solute
