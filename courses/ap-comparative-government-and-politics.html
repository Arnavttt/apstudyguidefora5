Q1
The six core AP® Comparative Government countries are
  • A) US, UK, France, Germany, Japan, Brazil
  • B) UK, France, Germany, China, India, South Africa
  • C) Russia, China, Mexico, Iran, Brazil, Nigeria
  • D) UK, Russia, China, Mexico, Iran, and Nigeria
D is correct. UK, Russia, China, Mexico, Iran, and Nigeria
Q2
A federal system of government distributes power
  • A) entirely to the central government
  • B) only to local governments
  • C) among political parties in proportion to their vote share
  • D) between a central government and regional/state governments, each with constitutionally defined authority
D is correct. between a central government and regional/state governments, each with constitutionally defined authority
Q3
China's political system is best described as
  • A) a federal democracy with competitive elections
  • B) a constitutional monarchy
  • C) a parliamentary democracy with CCP dominance
  • D) a single-party authoritarian state led by the Chinese Communist Party (CCP)
D is correct. a single-party authoritarian state led by the Chinese Communist Party (CCP)
Q4
A unitary state is one where
  • A) regional governments are independent of the center
  • B) the central government holds ultimate authority; subnational governments exercise only delegated powers
  • C) power is equally divided between two levels of government
  • D) the military controls the central government
B is correct. the central government holds ultimate authority; subnational governments exercise only delegated powers
Q5
Iran's government is characterized by
  • A) a theocratic republic with both elected institutions (president, parliament) and unelected clerical oversight (Supreme Leader)
  • B) a parliamentary democracy modeled on the UK
  • C) a military dictatorship with no elections
  • D) a secular federal republic
A is correct. a theocratic republic with both elected institutions (president, parliament) and unelected clerical oversight (Supreme Leader)
Q6
The UK's "fusion of powers" means
  • A) the House of Lords controls all legislation
  • B) the executive (prime minister and cabinet) is drawn from and accountable to the legislature (parliament)
  • C) there is no judicial review of laws
  • D) parliament and the monarchy share equal power
B is correct. the executive (prime minister and cabinet) is drawn from and accountable to the legislature (parliament)
Q7
Legitimacy in political science refers to
  • A) the military strength of a regime
  • B) the widely accepted belief that a government's authority is rightful and that citizens should obey it
  • C) the legal power to enforce laws by force
  • D) the number of votes a government received
B is correct. the widely accepted belief that a government's authority is rightful and that citizens should obey it
Q8
Nigeria's political system is a
  • A) federal presidential republic, modeled partly on the U.S. system, with a history of military coups and civilian rule alternating
  • B) unitary parliamentary state
  • C) constitutional monarchy
  • D) communist single-party state
A is correct. federal presidential republic, modeled partly on the U.S. system, with a history of military coups and civilian rule alternating
Q9
Mexico transitioned from one-party rule (PRI dominance) to electoral democracy primarily in
  • A) 1968, after the Tlatelolco massacre
  • B) 1994, after the Zapatista uprising
  • C) 2018, with López Obrador's election
  • D) 2000, when Vicente Fox (PAN) won the presidency, ending 71 years of PRI rule
D is correct. 2000, when Vicente Fox (PAN) won the presidency, ending 71 years of PRI rule
Q10
The concept of "civil society" refers to
  • A) all citizens regardless of their organizational membership
  • B) the network of voluntary organizations, NGOs, religious groups, and civic associations between the state and the individual
  • C) the formal institutions of government
  • D) the military and police forces
B is correct. the network of voluntary organizations, NGOs, religious groups, and civic associations between the state and the individual
Q11
Russia's political system under Putin is best described as
  • A) a stable liberal democracy
  • B) a complete totalitarian dictatorship with no elections
  • C) a genuine parliamentary system
  • D) a managed (illiberal) democracy or hybrid regime — formal elections exist but real competition is suppressed
D is correct. a managed (illiberal) democracy or hybrid regime — formal elections exist but real competition is suppressed
Q12
Electoral systems using proportional representation (PR) tend to produce
  • A) less voter turnout than majoritarian systems
  • B) stronger executive power
  • C) more political parties and coalition governments compared to first-past-the-post (winner-take-all) systems
  • D) two-party systems dominated by one major party
C is correct. more political parties and coalition governments compared to first-past-the-post (winner-take-all) systems
Q13
A command economy differs from a market economy in that the state
  • A) allows free competition but taxes all income equally
  • B) privatizes all state assets
  • C) centrally plans production, prices, and distribution rather than leaving them to market forces
  • D) only regulates environmental standards
C is correct. centrally plans production, prices, and distribution rather than leaving them to market forces
Q14
Corporatism in comparative politics refers to a system where
  • A) the state is owned by corporations
  • B) the state organizes and controls interest groups (labor, business) as formal partners in policy-making
  • C) corporations control the government
  • D) multiple competing interest groups pressure government independently (pluralism)
B is correct. the state organizes and controls interest groups (labor, business) as formal partners in policy-making
Q15
Judicial independence is important in democratic systems because it
  • A) ensures judges are elected by the people
  • B) gives courts control over the legislature
  • C) allows courts to check government power and protect rights without political interference
  • D) makes courts more efficient and faster
C is correct. allows courts to check government power and protect rights without political interference
Q16
The Duma is the lower house of
  • A) Mexico's legislature (Chamber of Deputies)
  • B) Russia's parliament (Federal Assembly)
  • C) China's National People's Congress
  • D) Iran's parliament (Majlis)
B is correct. Russia's parliament (Federal Assembly)
Q17
The concept of "presidentialism" vs "parliamentarism" refers to
  • A) whether the executive is directly elected and independent of the legislature (presidential) or draws power from legislative confidence (parliamentary)
  • B) whether the country has a president or prime minister as head of state (they can overlap)
  • C) the strength of judicial review in a system
  • D) the degree of federalism in a country
A is correct. whether the executive is directly elected and independent of the legislature (presidential) or draws power from legislative confidence (parliamentary)
Q18
Social cleavages in comparative politics are
  • A) differences in electoral systems between countries
  • B) divisions within society (e.g., ethnic, religious, regional, class) that shape political alignments and conflict
  • C) constitutional provisions that separate powers
  • D) gaps in income between social classes only
B is correct. divisions within society (e.g., ethnic, religious, regional, class) that shape political alignments and conflict
Q19
The National People's Congress (NPC) of China is best described as
  • A) an elected body with genuine opposition parties
  • B) the Chinese equivalent of the U.S. Senate
  • C) a largely rubber-stamp legislature that ratifies CCP decisions; it lacks real independent legislative power
  • D) the true decision-making body of the Chinese government
C is correct. a largely rubber-stamp legislature that ratifies CCP decisions; it lacks real independent legislative power
Q20
Regime change (democratization) is most likely to occur when
  • A) the GDP growth rate exceeds 5% annually
  • B) economic crises undermine authoritarian legitimacy, civil society pressures increase, and elites fracture
  • C) the military publicly declares support for democracy
  • D) the country joins international organizations
B is correct. economic crises undermine authoritarian legitimacy, civil society pressures increase, and elites fracture
