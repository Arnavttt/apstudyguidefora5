Q1
The AP® Language exam primarily tests students' ability to
  • A) identify grammar errors in published works
  • B) memorize literary terms
  • C) analyze rhetorical strategies in nonfiction prose and write effective arguments
  • D) summarize fiction and poetry
C is correct. analyze rhetorical strategies in nonfiction prose and write effective arguments
Q2
Ethos is an appeal to
  • A) the audience's self-interest
  • B) credibility or ethical character — establishing the speaker's trustworthiness or authority
  • C) emotion
  • D) logic and reason
B is correct. credibility or ethical character — establishing the speaker's trustworthiness or authority
Q3
Pathos is an appeal to
  • A) credibility and character
  • B) logic and evidence
  • C) shared values without emotional language
  • D) emotion — evoking feelings in the audience to support the argument
D is correct. emotion — evoking feelings in the audience to support the argument
Q4
A rhetorical situation includes
  • A) speaker, audience, purpose, context, and the exigence (the reason the text was written)
  • B) only the author's purpose and the text itself
  • C) the historical period and literary genre
  • D) the thesis, evidence, and conclusion
A is correct. speaker, audience, purpose, context, and the exigence (the reason the text was written)
Q5
In argument analysis, a concession is when the author
  • A) agrees with the opposing side entirely
  • B) ignores counterarguments to strengthen their case
  • C) uses emotional language to dismiss opponents
  • D) acknowledges an opposing viewpoint or limitation of their argument, then counters it (builds credibility)
D is correct. acknowledges an opposing viewpoint or limitation of their argument, then counters it (builds credibility)
Q6
Anaphora is a rhetorical device defined as
  • A) an indirect reference to a cultural text
  • B) the repetition of a word or phrase at the beginning of successive clauses for emphasis and rhythm
  • C) the repetition of a word at the end of successive clauses
  • D) a direct comparison using "like" or "as"
B is correct. the repetition of a word or phrase at the beginning of successive clauses for emphasis and rhythm
Q7
Epistrophe is the repetition of words or phrases
  • A) in a chiastic pattern (A-B-B-A)
  • B) at the end of successive clauses (opposite of anaphora)
  • C) at the beginning of clauses
  • D) throughout a text at irregular intervals
B is correct. at the end of successive clauses (opposite of anaphora)
Q8
The rhetorical triangle (Aristotle's rhetorical appeals) consists of
  • A) ethos (credibility), pathos (emotion), and logos (logic)
  • B) speaker, audience, and purpose only
  • C) narration, argument, and refutation
  • D) claim, evidence, and warrant
A is correct. ethos (credibility), pathos (emotion), and logos (logic)
Q9
In a synthesis essay, students are expected to
  • A) write only from personal experience and analysis
  • B) develop their own argument and use at least three provided sources as evidence to support it
  • C) summarize each source without taking a position
  • D) find the best source and summarize it fully
B is correct. develop their own argument and use at least three provided sources as evidence to support it
Q10
Diction refers to
  • A) the use of figurative language only
  • B) the argument's logical structure
  • C) word choice — the specific vocabulary, tone, and level of formality a writer uses to achieve effect
  • D) sentence length and structure (syntax)
C is correct. word choice — the specific vocabulary, tone, and level of formality a writer uses to achieve effect
Q11
Syntax in rhetorical analysis refers to
  • A) sentence structure — how the arrangement of words and clauses affects rhythm, emphasis, and meaning
  • B) the vocabulary and word choice of the text
  • C) the use of sound devices like alliteration
  • D) the logical structure of the argument
A is correct. sentence structure — how the arrangement of words and clauses affects rhythm, emphasis, and meaning
Q12
Chiasmus is a rhetorical device in which
  • A) two clauses or phrases are structured in reverse grammatical order (A-B : B-A structure)
  • B) a statement is repeated for emphasis
  • C) a word is left out to create ambiguity
  • D) the author speaks to an absent person
A is correct. two clauses or phrases are structured in reverse grammatical order (A-B : B-A structure)
Q13
The difference between "argument" and "persuasion" in AP® Lang is that argument
  • A) relies primarily on logos and reason, aiming to convince through evidence and logic rather than purely emotional appeal
  • B) ignores the audience entirely
  • C) uses only ethos and pathos
  • D) requires no evidence, only opinion
A is correct. relies primarily on logos and reason, aiming to convince through evidence and logic rather than purely emotional appeal
Q14
An ad hominem fallacy attacks
  • A) the person making the argument rather than the argument itself
  • B) the credibility of the evidence used
  • C) the logic of the conclusion
  • D) an extreme version of the opposing position (strawman)
A is correct. the person making the argument rather than the argument itself
Q15
In an AP® Language rhetorical analysis essay, the thesis should
  • A) summarize the main idea of the passage
  • B) identify the audience and speaker without analysis
  • C) make a defensible claim about how the author's rhetorical choices work together to achieve a specific purpose or effect
  • D) list all rhetorical devices used in the passage
C is correct. make a defensible claim about how the author's rhetorical choices work together to achieve a specific purpose or effect
Q16
A Rogerian argument strategy involves
  • A) presenting only one side of an issue
  • B) acknowledging the legitimacy of opposing views and finding common ground before presenting one's own position
  • C) directly attacking the opposing side's credibility
  • D) using only emotional appeals to win the audience
B is correct. acknowledging the legitimacy of opposing views and finding common ground before presenting one's own position
Q17
A periodic sentence builds toward its main clause at the end, creating
  • A) repetition for emotional emphasis
  • B) parallel structure throughout
  • C) suspense and emphasis by withholding the main idea until the conclusion
  • D) clarity by stating the main idea first
C is correct. suspense and emphasis by withholding the main idea until the conclusion
Q18
The term "exigence" in rhetoric refers to
  • A) the urgent problem or situation that calls the text into being — the reason the author writes
  • B) the audience's prior knowledge
  • C) the author's credibility and expertise
  • D) the emotional tone of the piece
A is correct. the urgent problem or situation that calls the text into being — the reason the author writes
Q19
A hasty generalization fallacy occurs when
  • A) the argument attacks the speaker rather than the claim
  • B) two events are assumed to be causally related
  • C) evidence is cited without proper attribution
  • D) a broad conclusion is drawn from too few or unrepresentative examples
D is correct. a broad conclusion is drawn from too few or unrepresentative examples
Q20
In the AP® Language argument essay, students should
  • A) develop a nuanced, evidence-supported position on an issue, acknowledging complexity and using reasoning to persuade
  • B) summarize three outside sources to support a thesis
  • C) write a creative narrative about a personal experience
  • D) identify rhetorical devices in a provided passage
A is correct. develop a nuanced, evidence-supported position on an issue, acknowledging complexity and using reasoning to persuade
