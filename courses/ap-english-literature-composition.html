Q1
The AP® Literature exam assesses close reading primarily through
  • A) summaries of novels read at home
  • B) Multiple-choice questions on poetry and prose passages, plus three free-response essays
  • C) grammar and vocabulary tests
  • D) research papers on literary history
B is correct. Multiple-choice questions on poetry and prose passages, plus three free-response essays
Q2
A literary motif differs from a theme in that a motif is
  • A) the climax of the plot
  • B) a recurring image, symbol, or situation that reinforces meaning, while a theme is the central idea or message
  • C) the central argument of the work
  • D) the narrator's point of view
B is correct. a recurring image, symbol, or situation that reinforces meaning, while a theme is the central idea or message
Q3
In media res is a narrative technique where the story begins
  • A) with a detailed setting description
  • B) from the antagonist's perspective
  • C) in the middle of the action, with background filled in later through flashback or exposition
  • D) at the beginning of the protagonist's life
C is correct. in the middle of the action, with background filled in later through flashback or exposition
Q4
Free indirect discourse is a narrative technique in which
  • A) dialogue is presented without attribution
  • B) the narrator presents a character's thoughts using third-person voice, blending narrator and character perspectives
  • C) the narrator speaks directly to the reader as "you"
  • D) a character narrates in first person
B is correct. the narrator presents a character's thoughts using third-person voice, blending narrator and character perspectives
Q5
An unreliable narrator is one whose account the reader should
  • A) ignore in favor of other characters
  • B) treat as the author's own voice
  • C) question, because the narrator's perspective is limited, biased, or self-deceptive
  • D) trust completely because they are the protagonist
C is correct. question, because the narrator's perspective is limited, biased, or self-deceptive
Q6
Dramatic irony occurs when
  • A) the audience knows something the characters in the story do not
  • B) a character says the opposite of what they mean
  • C) events turn out opposite to what was expected
  • D) the setting contradicts the mood
A is correct. the audience knows something the characters in the story do not
Q7
The term "foil" refers to a character who
  • A) provides comic relief
  • B) shares all traits with the protagonist
  • C) contrasts with the protagonist to highlight the protagonist's traits more clearly
  • D) opposes the protagonist as the main villain
C is correct. contrasts with the protagonist to highlight the protagonist's traits more clearly
Q8
In poetry, a caesura is
  • A) the repetition of consonant sounds
  • B) a shift in the poem's tone or argument
  • C) a strong pause within a line of verse, often marked by punctuation
  • D) the final couplet of a Shakespearean sonnet
C is correct. a strong pause within a line of verse, often marked by punctuation
Q9
The term "volta" in poetry refers to
  • A) the rhyme scheme of a poem
  • B) a turn or shift in argument, tone, or subject, especially in sonnets
  • C) a pause at the end of a line
  • D) the repetition of a phrase for emphasis
B is correct. a turn or shift in argument, tone, or subject, especially in sonnets
Q10
An extended metaphor that runs throughout a poem or passage is called a(n)
  • A) synecdoche
  • B) conceit
  • C) simile
  • D) allegory
B is correct. conceit
Q11
Catharsis, as described by Aristotle, is the
  • A) flaw that leads to the hero's downfall
  • B) resolution of conflict at the end of a play
  • C) emotional purging or release felt by the audience at the end of a tragedy
  • D) protagonist's moment of recognition (anagnorisis)
C is correct. emotional purging or release felt by the audience at the end of a tragedy
Q12
The narrative technique of stream of consciousness attempts to
  • A) shift between multiple narrators chapter by chapter
  • B) provide a reliable, omniscient account
  • C) replicate the continuous, often fragmented flow of a character's inner thoughts
  • D) present events in strictly chronological order
C is correct. replicate the continuous, often fragmented flow of a character's inner thoughts
Q13
When an author uses situational irony, the outcome
  • A) is revealed only at the very end
  • B) is the opposite of what was expected or would seem appropriate
  • C) is exactly what a character predicted
  • D) involves a character saying the opposite of what they mean
B is correct. is the opposite of what was expected or would seem appropriate
Q14
A symbol in literature is
  • A) an object, person, or event that represents both itself and a broader abstract meaning
  • B) a direct comparison using "like" or "as"
  • C) a figure of speech substituting one word for another
  • D) the central conflict of the plot
A is correct. an object, person, or event that represents both itself and a broader abstract meaning
Q15
Synecdoche uses
  • A) an indirect reference to a well-known person or event
  • B) a part to represent the whole, or the whole to represent a part
  • C) a word that sounds like what it represents
  • D) exaggeration for effect
B is correct. a part to represent the whole, or the whole to represent a part
Q16
A Bildungsroman is a novel focused on
  • A) the political satire of a society
  • B) an episodic adventure with no character growth
  • C) the moral and psychological growth of the protagonist from youth to adulthood
  • D) the decline of a tragic hero
C is correct. the moral and psychological growth of the protagonist from youth to adulthood
Q17
The term "diction" in literary analysis refers to
  • A) sentence length and structure
  • B) the use of figurative language
  • C) the rhyme and meter of poetry
  • D) a writer's word choice and how it contributes to tone and meaning
D is correct. a writer's word choice and how it contributes to tone and meaning
Q18
In a Shakespearean sonnet, the resolution or "turn" typically occurs in the
  • A) opening two lines
  • B) final couplet (last two lines)
  • C) first quatrain
  • D) third quatrain
B is correct. final couplet (last two lines)
Q19
Pathetic fallacy is a literary device in which
  • A) an author uses extreme emotion to persuade the reader
  • B) characters behave irrationally due to passion
  • C) the narrator directly addresses the reader
  • D) the natural environment reflects or mirrors the emotional state of a character
D is correct. the natural environment reflects or mirrors the emotional state of a character
Q20
In close reading, the term "syntax" refers to
  • A) vocabulary and word choice
  • B) the use of sound devices like rhyme
  • C) the narrative point of view
  • D) sentence structure — how clauses, phrases, and words are arranged — and its effect on meaning and rhythm
D is correct. sentence structure — how clauses, phrases, and words are arranged — and its effect on meaning and rhythm
