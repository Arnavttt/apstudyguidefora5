Q1
The 10% rule in ecology states that
  • A) only about 10% of energy is transferred to the next trophic level; the rest is lost as heat
  • B) 10% of species in an ecosystem are keystone species
  • C) photosynthesis converts 10% of solar energy to glucose
  • D) 10% of water in the hydrologic cycle evaporates
A is correct. only about 10% of energy is transferred to the next trophic level; the rest is lost as heat
Q2
Which greenhouse gas has the highest global warming potential per molecule?
  • A) Oxygen (O₂)
  • B) Nitrous oxide (N₂O) and fluorinated gases have higher GWP than CO₂, though CO₂ is most abundant
  • C) Water vapor (H₂O)
  • D) Carbon dioxide (CO₂)
B is correct. Nitrous oxide (N₂O) and fluorinated gases have higher GWP than CO₂, though CO₂ is most abundant
Q3
The IPAT equation describes environmental impact as
  • A) I = Pollution + Adaptation + Temperature
  • B) I = Income × Population × Area × Time
  • C) I = Industry × Population × Agriculture
  • D) Impact = Population × Affluence × Technology (resource use per unit of production)
D is correct. Impact = Population × Affluence × Technology (resource use per unit of production)
Q4
Eutrophication in water bodies is caused by
  • A) introduction of invasive species
  • B) thermal pollution from power plants
  • C) excess nutrient runoff (nitrogen, phosphorus) that causes algal blooms, oxygen depletion, and fish kills
  • D) acid rain reducing pH below 5
C is correct. excess nutrient runoff (nitrogen, phosphorus) that causes algal blooms, oxygen depletion, and fish kills
Q5
Ozone depletion in the stratosphere is primarily caused by
  • A) carbon dioxide accumulation in the upper atmosphere
  • B) methane from agricultural activities
  • C) nitrogen oxides from vehicle emissions
  • D) chlorofluorocarbons (CFCs) releasing chlorine atoms that catalytically destroy ozone molecules
D is correct. chlorofluorocarbons (CFCs) releasing chlorine atoms that catalytically destroy ozone molecules
Q6
The Coriolis effect influences global wind patterns by
  • A) causing warm air to rise at the equator
  • B) reversing trade winds seasonally
  • C) deflecting moving air to the right in the Northern Hemisphere and left in the Southern Hemisphere due to Earth's rotation
  • D) creating thermal gradients between land and ocean
C is correct. deflecting moving air to the right in the Northern Hemisphere and left in the Southern Hemisphere due to Earth's rotation
Q7
Primary succession occurs on
  • A) bare, lifeless substrate (e.g., bare rock, lava flows) with no existing soil
  • B) disturbed land that still has soil and seed banks
  • C) agricultural fields after harvest
  • D) mature forests after selective logging
A is correct. bare, lifeless substrate (e.g., bare rock, lava flows) with no existing soil
Q8
The tragedy of the commons describes
  • A) how individuals overexploit shared (common) resources because each gains full benefit while costs are shared
  • B) the inefficiency of government-owned land
  • C) pollution from tragedy-causing industrial accidents
  • D) the loss of biodiversity in national parks
A is correct. how individuals overexploit shared (common) resources because each gains full benefit while costs are shared
Q9
Cap-and-trade programs reduce pollution by
  • A) setting a total pollution limit and allowing polluters to buy and sell permits, incentivizing the cheapest reductions first
  • B) taxing all carbon emissions equally regardless of output
  • C) banning the most polluting industries
  • D) requiring all firms to use the same technology
A is correct. setting a total pollution limit and allowing polluters to buy and sell permits, incentivizing the cheapest reductions first
Q10
Integrated pest management (IPM) differs from conventional pesticide use in that IPM
  • A) eliminates all pest control measures
  • B) uses only genetically modified crops
  • C) combines biological, cultural, mechanical, and chemical controls to minimize pesticide use while managing pests effectively
  • D) relies exclusively on chemical pesticides
C is correct. combines biological, cultural, mechanical, and chemical controls to minimize pesticide use while managing pests effectively
Q11
The hydrologic cycle moves water primarily through
  • A) tidal forces and ocean currents only
  • B) volcanic outgassing and plate tectonics
  • C) evaporation, condensation, precipitation, surface runoff, infiltration, and transpiration
  • D) photosynthesis, respiration, and nitrogen fixation
C is correct. evaporation, condensation, precipitation, surface runoff, infiltration, and transpiration
Q12
Soil horizons from top to bottom are
  • A) O (organic), A (topsoil), E (eluviation), B (subsoil), C (parent material), R (bedrock)
  • B) A, B, C, D, E from surface to bedrock
  • C) humus, clay, sand, gravel, bedrock
  • D) topsoil, subsoil, clay pan, parent rock
A is correct. O (organic), A (topsoil), E (eluviation), B (subsoil), C (parent material), R (bedrock)
Q13
The Safe Drinking Water Act (SDWA) in the U.S.
  • A) sets maximum contaminant levels for drinking water and requires public water system testing and reporting
  • B) bans all industrial discharge into rivers and lakes
  • C) requires states to reduce groundwater extraction
  • D) regulates air quality near water sources
A is correct. sets maximum contaminant levels for drinking water and requires public water system testing and reporting
Q14
Biomagnification refers to
  • A) the accumulation of nutrients in soil over time
  • B) growth in population size over multiple generations
  • C) the increasing size of organisms at higher trophic levels
  • D) the increasing concentration of persistent pollutants (e.g., DDT, mercury) at higher trophic levels
D is correct. the increasing concentration of persistent pollutants (e.g., DDT, mercury) at higher trophic levels
Q15
ENSO (El Niño-Southern Oscillation) events affect global climate by
  • A) changing the tilt of Earth's axis seasonally
  • B) increasing volcanic activity in the Pacific Ring of Fire
  • C) shifting warm Pacific waters eastward (El Niño) or westward (La Niña), altering precipitation and temperature patterns globally
  • D) reversing Earth's rotation during peak solar years
C is correct. shifting warm Pacific waters eastward (El Niño) or westward (La Niña), altering precipitation and temperature patterns globally
Q16
The Clean Air Act regulates
  • A) six criteria air pollutants including particulate matter, ozone, CO, NO₂, SO₂, and lead, and sets National Ambient Air Quality Standards
  • B) only indoor air quality in workplaces
  • C) greenhouse gas emissions only
  • D) water quality near industrial facilities
A is correct. six criteria air pollutants including particulate matter, ozone, CO, NO₂, SO₂, and lead, and sets National Ambient Air Quality Standards
Q17
A keystone species is one that
  • A) is the most abundant species in an ecosystem
  • B) is only found in one geographic location
  • C) performs photosynthesis as the primary producer
  • D) has a disproportionately large effect on its ecosystem relative to its abundance, and its removal causes dramatic ecological change
D is correct. has a disproportionately large effect on its ecosystem relative to its abundance, and its removal causes dramatic ecological change
Q18
Non-renewable energy sources differ from renewable sources in that they
  • A) cannot be used to generate electricity
  • B) always produce more pollution than renewable sources
  • C) are only found in developing countries
  • D) form over millions of years and are depleted faster than they can be replenished
D is correct. form over millions of years and are depleted faster than they can be replenished
Q19

  • A) banned ocean dumping of plastic globally

  • A) average population size over any 10-year period
