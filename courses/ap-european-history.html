Q1
The Renaissance began in Italy primarily because of
  • A) papal authority encouraging secular art
  • B) the wealth of Italian city-states, access to classical texts, and trade networks that brought new ideas
  • C) the Black Death destroying feudal institutions
  • D) the Crusades introducing Italian scholars to Arab science
B is correct. the wealth of Italian city-states, access to classical texts, and trade networks that brought new ideas
Q2
The Peace of Westphalia (1648) is considered foundational because it
  • A) ended the French Revolution
  • B) established the principle of state sovereignty and ended the Thirty Years' War
  • C) created a unified German state
  • D) established religious freedom throughout Europe
B is correct. established the principle of state sovereignty and ended the Thirty Years' War
Q3
The Scientific Revolution challenged medieval thought primarily by
  • A) ending university education based on ancient texts
  • B) developing the steam engine and industrial technology
  • C) replacing Aristotelian cosmology and scholasticism with observation, experimentation, and mathematical laws
  • D) promoting religious skepticism and atheism across Europe
C is correct. replacing Aristotelian cosmology and scholasticism with observation, experimentation, and mathematical laws
Q4
The French Revolution's radical phase (Reign of Terror) was characterized by
  • A) Napoleon's military dictatorship
  • B) royalist counter-revolution
  • C) economic prosperity from colonial trade
  • D) mass executions of perceived enemies of the republic under Robespierre's Committee of Public Safety
D is correct. mass executions of perceived enemies of the republic under Robespierre's Committee of Public Safety
Q5
Napoleon's Continental System attempted to
  • A) create a common European currency
  • B) end the slave trade in French colonies
  • C) blockade Britain economically by forbidding European nations from trading with Britain
  • D) unite Europe under French administrative law
C is correct. blockade Britain economically by forbidding European nations from trading with Britain
Q6
The Congress of Vienna (1815) sought to restore
  • A) the French monarchy with new constitutional limits
  • B) the Holy Roman Empire under Habsburg rule
  • C) the conservative political order and balance of power in Europe after the Napoleonic Wars
  • D) democratic revolutions in all major states
C is correct. the conservative political order and balance of power in Europe after the Napoleonic Wars
Q7
The revolutions of 1848 across Europe were primarily driven by
  • A) colonial uprisings against European empires
  • B) liberal nationalism, economic hardship, and middle-class demands for constitutional government
  • C) communist movements led by Karl Marx
  • D) religious conflicts between Catholics and Protestants
B is correct. liberal nationalism, economic hardship, and middle-class demands for constitutional government
Q8
Otto von Bismarck's "Realpolitik" approach to German unification involved
  • A) appealing to German cultural unity through propaganda only
  • B) using wars against Denmark, Austria, and France to forge a Prussian-dominated German state through pragmatic power politics
  • C) democratic elections across German states
  • D) peaceful negotiation and economic integration
B is correct. using wars against Denmark, Austria, and France to forge a Prussian-dominated German state through pragmatic power politics
Q9
The "New Imperialism" of the late 19th century was driven by
  • A) declining European military power
  • B) industrial capitalism's need for raw materials and markets, nationalism, and Social Darwinian ideology
  • C) purely religious missionary motives
  • D) the need to resettle excess European population
B is correct. industrial capitalism's need for raw materials and markets, nationalism, and Social Darwinian ideology
Q10
World War I's Western Front became a stalemate because
  • A) peace negotiations continually interrupted combat
  • B) defensive technology (machine guns, barbed wire, artillery) gave defenders a decisive advantage over offensive attacks
  • C) both sides ran out of soldiers
  • D) American neutrality allowed Germany to consolidate positions
B is correct. defensive technology (machine guns, barbed wire, artillery) gave defenders a decisive advantage over offensive attacks
Q11
The Bolshevik Revolution succeeded in 1917 largely because
  • A) Germany directly invaded Russia to install Lenin
  • B) Britain and France refused to support the Provisional Government
  • C) the Provisional Government continued the unpopular war, Lenin promised peace, land, and bread, and the Bolsheviks organized workers and soldiers effectively
  • D) the Tsar voluntarily abdicated to the Bolsheviks
C is correct. the Provisional Government continued the unpopular war, Lenin promised peace, land, and bread, and the Bolsheviks organized workers and soldiers effectively
Q12
The Great Depression's political impact in Europe included
  • A) the rise of fascist and authoritarian movements exploiting mass unemployment and social despair
  • B) strengthening of democratic institutions across Europe
  • C) renewed socialist internationalism
  • D) the end of colonial empires
A is correct. the rise of fascist and authoritarian movements exploiting mass unemployment and social despair
Q13
Nazi Germany's racial ideology was rooted in
  • A) Marxist class struggle theory
  • B) traditional Christian theology
  • C) Italian fascist corporatism
  • D) Social Darwinism, antisemitism, and pan-German nationalism, used to justify genocide and conquest
D is correct. Social Darwinism, antisemitism, and pan-German nationalism, used to justify genocide and conquest
Q14
The Marshall Plan (1948) was significant because it
  • A) provided U.S. economic aid to rebuild Western European economies, tying them to American capitalism and opposing Soviet expansion
  • B) created NATO's military alliance structure
  • C) reunified Germany after WWII
  • D) established the European Union
A is correct. provided U.S. economic aid to rebuild Western European economies, tying them to American capitalism and opposing Soviet expansion
Q15
Decolonization after WWII was accelerated in Europe by
  • A) religious uprisings within European capitals
  • B) weakened European powers, colonial nationalist movements, Cold War pressures, and UN anti-colonial resolutions
  • C) unanimous European agreement to grant independence peacefully
  • D) U.S. military liberation of all colonies
B is correct. weakened European powers, colonial nationalist movements, Cold War pressures, and UN anti-colonial resolutions
Q16
The European Union evolved from
  • A) the Congress of Vienna's political framework
  • B) Churchill's United States of Europe speech alone
  • C) the European Coal and Steel Community (1951), which began economic integration to prevent future European wars
  • D) a postwar NATO military alliance
C is correct. the European Coal and Steel Community (1951), which began economic integration to prevent future European wars
Q17
The "Second Industrial Revolution" (1870s–1914) differed from the first in that it
  • A) relied primarily on slave labor
  • B) featured steel, chemicals, electricity, and scientific management, with Germany and the U.S. surpassing Britain
  • C) was driven by water-powered textile factories
  • D) occurred only in Britain and France
B is correct. featured steel, chemicals, electricity, and scientific management, with Germany and the U.S. surpassing Britain
Q18
Appeasement in the 1930s (Munich Agreement, 1938) failed to prevent WWII because
  • A) France and Britain did not offer Germany enough territory
  • B) the Soviet Union rejected the deal
  • C) the League of Nations vetoed British appeasement
  • D) Hitler's demands were unlimited — each concession encouraged further aggression rather than satisfying German goals
D is correct. Hitler's demands were unlimited — each concession encouraged further aggression rather than satisfying German goals
Q19
The Cold War division of Europe created
  • A) NATO (Western alliance) vs. Warsaw Pact (Soviet bloc), with the Iron Curtain dividing capitalist West from communist East
  • B) a unified European defense force
  • C) immediate nuclear war between superpowers
  • D) democratic revolutions in all Eastern bloc nations
A is correct. NATO (Western alliance) vs. Warsaw Pact (Soviet bloc), with the Iron Curtain dividing capitalist West from communist East
Q20
The fall of the Berlin Wall in 1989 symbolized
  • A) the dissolution of the Soviet Union itself
  • B) a temporary crisis in East German politics
  • C) the collapse of communist governments across Eastern Europe and the end of the Cold War division of Europe
  • D) the beginning of German unification negotiations only
C is correct. the collapse of communist governments across Eastern Europe and the end of the Cold War division of Europe
