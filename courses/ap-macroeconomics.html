AP® Macroeconomics — 20 course-level questions0 / 20 correct
Q1
GDP is best defined as
  • A) total government spending plus private investment
  • B) total exports minus total imports
  • C) the total market value of all final goods and services produced within a country in a given year
  • D) total income earned by a nation's citizens wherever they live
C is correct. the total market value of all final goods and services produced within a country in a given year
Q2
The expenditure approach to GDP is expressed as
  • A) GDP = C + I + G + (X − M)
  • B) GDP = wages + rent + interest + profit
  • C) GDP = consumer spending + tax revenue
  • D) GDP = national income + depreciation
A is correct. GDP = C + I + G + (X − M)
Q3
If the consumer price index (CPI) rises from 150 to 165, the inflation rate is
  • A) 1.5%
  • B) 65%
  • C) 10%
  • D) 15%
C is correct. 10%
Q4
Cyclical unemployment is caused by
  • A) insufficient aggregate demand during an economic downturn
  • B) workers changing jobs voluntarily
  • C) structural changes in the economy
  • D) seasonal fluctuations in labor demand
A is correct. insufficient aggregate demand during an economic downturn
Q5
According to the classical model, the long-run aggregate supply curve is
  • A) vertical at the full-employment output level
  • B) upward sloping
  • C) downward sloping
  • D) horizontal at the price level
A is correct. vertical at the full-employment output level
Q6
An expansionary fiscal policy involves
  • A) increasing government spending and/or cutting taxes to boost aggregate demand
  • B) raising taxes and cutting spending to reduce the deficit
  • C) raising the federal funds rate
  • D) selling government bonds on the open market
A is correct. increasing government spending and/or cutting taxes to boost aggregate demand
Q7
The money multiplier is calculated as
  • A) 1 ÷ required reserve ratio
  • B) required reserves ÷ total deposits
  • C) money supply ÷ nominal GDP
  • D) nominal GDP ÷ velocity of money
A is correct. 1 ÷ required reserve ratio
Q8
When the Fed buys government bonds on the open market, the result is
  • A) an increase in the required reserve ratio
  • B) higher inflation expectations only
  • C) an increase in the money supply and lower interest rates
  • D) a decrease in the money supply and higher interest rates
C is correct. an increase in the money supply and lower interest rates
Q9
The Phillips Curve illustrates the short-run relationship between
  • A) inflation and unemployment (inverse relationship)
  • B) GDP growth and unemployment
  • C) interest rates and investment
  • D) the money supply and price level
A is correct. inflation and unemployment (inverse relationship)
Q10
Crowding out occurs when government borrowing
  • A) reduces consumer confidence immediately
  • B) raises interest rates, which reduces private investment
  • C) decreases the money supply directly
  • D) causes inflation by printing money
B is correct. raises interest rates, which reduces private investment
Q11
A current account surplus means a country
  • A) imports more than it exports
  • B) has more foreign investment flowing in than out
  • C) has a budget surplus
  • D) exports more goods, services, and income than it imports
D is correct. exports more goods, services, and income than it imports
Q12
In the loanable funds market, an increase in the government budget deficit will
  • A) have no effect if Ricardian equivalence holds
  • B) reduce private savings directly
  • C) shift the demand for loanable funds right, raising the real interest rate
  • D) shift supply of loanable funds right, lowering the rate
C is correct. shift the demand for loanable funds right, raising the real interest rate
Q13
The natural rate of unemployment equals
  • A) the unemployment rate during a recession
  • B) frictional unemployment plus structural unemployment
  • C) zero unemployment
  • D) cyclical unemployment plus frictional
B is correct. frictional unemployment plus structural unemployment
Q14
Nominal GDP differs from real GDP in that nominal GDP
  • A) is always higher than real GDP
  • B) is measured at current prices (not adjusted for inflation)
  • C) excludes government spending
  • D) measures production per capita
B is correct. is measured at current prices (not adjusted for inflation)
Q15
Cost-push inflation is caused by
  • A) increases in the money multiplier
  • B) a decrease in short-run aggregate supply (e.g., rising input costs)
  • C) excessive demand from consumers and businesses
  • D) expansionary monetary policy alone
B is correct. a decrease in short-run aggregate supply (e.g., rising input costs)
Q16
The velocity of money (V) in the quantity theory of money (MV = PQ) represents
  • A) the interest rate charged by banks
  • B) the rate of inflation
  • C) the average number of times a dollar is spent on final goods in a year
  • D) the growth rate of the money supply
C is correct. the average number of times a dollar is spent on final goods in a year
Q17
A contractionary monetary policy aims to
  • A) depreciate the currency to boost exports
  • B) reduce inflation by decreasing the money supply and raising interest rates
  • C) stimulate growth by lowering reserve requirements
  • D) increase government spending during a recession
B is correct. reduce inflation by decreasing the money supply and raising interest rates
Q18
If the U.S. dollar appreciates relative to the euro, U.S. exports will likely
  • A) remain unchanged because trade balances are fixed
  • B) increase because U.S. production costs fall
  • C) decrease because American goods become more expensive for foreign buyers
  • D) increase because foreigners can afford more dollars
C is correct. decrease because American goods become more expensive for foreign buyers
Q19
Automatic stabilizers include
  • A) unemployment insurance and progressive income taxes, which act without legislative action
  • B) discretionary tax cuts passed by Congress
  • C) open market operations by the Fed
  • D) changes in the required reserve ratio
A is correct. unemployment insurance and progressive income taxes, which act without legislative action
Q20
The sacrifice ratio in macroeconomics refers to
  • A) the percentage of GDP lost for each 1 percentage point reduction in inflation
  • B) the cost of reducing unemployment by 1 percentage point
  • C) the trade-off between savings and consumption
  • D) the budget deficit required to achieve full employment
A is correct. the percentage of GDP lost for each 1 percentage point reduction in inflation
