Q1
Gauss's Law states that the electric flux through a closed surface equals
  • A) the line integral of E around the surface
  • B) the total enclosed charge divided by the permittivity of free space: Φ_E = Q_enc / ε₀
  • C) the surface area times the field magnitude
  • D) the work done by the electric field
B is correct. the total enclosed charge divided by the permittivity of free space: Φ_E = Q_enc / ε₀
Q2
The electric field inside a conductor in electrostatic equilibrium is
  • A) proportional to the surface charge density
  • B) directed radially outward from the center
  • C) zero — free charges redistribute until no net field exists inside
  • D) uniform and parallel to the surface
C is correct. zero — free charges redistribute until no net field exists inside
Q3
The capacitance of a parallel-plate capacitor is
  • A) C = ε₀A/d — proportional to plate area and inversely proportional to plate separation
  • B) C = Q × V
  • C) C = ε₀d/A
  • D) C = V/Q²
A is correct. C = ε₀A/d — proportional to plate area and inversely proportional to plate separation
Q4
When capacitors are connected in parallel, the equivalent capacitance is
  • A) the average of the individual capacitances
  • B) the sum of individual capacitances: C_eq = C₁ + C₂ + ...
  • C) less than the smallest individual capacitance
  • D) 1/C_eq = 1/C₁ + 1/C₂
B is correct. the sum of individual capacitances: C_eq = C₁ + C₂ + ...
Q5
Kirchhoff's Voltage Law (KVL) states that
  • A) the sum of currents into a node equals zero
  • B) voltage is constant throughout any loop
  • C) the voltage across each element is equal
  • D) the sum of all voltage drops around any closed loop equals zero (energy conservation)
D is correct. the sum of all voltage drops around any closed loop equals zero (energy conservation)
Q6
The magnetic force on a moving charge q in field B is given by
  • A) F = qE (using the electric field)
  • B) F = q|v|²/r
  • C) F = qv × B — perpendicular to both v and B, with magnitude qvB sinθ
  • D) F = qvB cosθ
C is correct. F = qv × B — perpendicular to both v and B, with magnitude qvB sinθ
Q7
Ampere's Law relates the magnetic field along a closed path to
  • A) the magnetic flux through the path
  • B) the total current enclosed by that path: ∮ B · dl = μ₀ I_enc
  • C) the total charge enclosed
  • D) the electric flux through the surface
B is correct. the total current enclosed by that path: ∮ B · dl = μ₀ I_enc
Q8
Faraday's Law of Induction states that the induced EMF is
  • A) ε = μ₀I/2πr
  • B) ε = −dΦ_B/dt — the negative rate of change of magnetic flux through a circuit
  • C) ε = BLv always
  • D) ε = Q/C
B is correct. ε = −dΦ_B/dt — the negative rate of change of magnetic flux through a circuit
Q9
Lenz's Law states that the induced current flows in a direction that
  • A) is always clockwise
  • B) opposes the change in magnetic flux that caused it (conservation of energy)
  • C) reinforces the magnetic flux change
  • D) maximizes the induced EMF
B is correct. opposes the change in magnetic flux that caused it (conservation of energy)
Q10
The energy stored in an inductor with inductance L carrying current I is
  • A) U = LI
  • B) U = (1/2)I²/L
  • C) U = LV/I
  • D) U = (1/2)LI²
D is correct. U = (1/2)LI²
Q11
In an RC circuit, the time constant τ represents
  • A) τ = R/C
  • B) the frequency of oscillation
  • C) τ = RC — the time for the capacitor to charge to about 63% of its final voltage
  • D) the time to fully charge or discharge the capacitor
C is correct. τ = RC — the time for the capacitor to charge to about 63% of its final voltage
Q12
Maxwell's addition to Ampere's Law introduced the concept of
  • A) displacement current — a changing electric flux creates a magnetic field even without charge flow
  • B) magnetic monopoles
  • C) the speed of light depending on medium
  • D) charge conservation in circuits
A is correct. displacement current — a changing electric flux creates a magnetic field even without charge flow
Q13
The electric potential V at a distance r from a point charge q is
  • A) V = qr/k
  • B) V = E/r
  • C) V = kq/r — potential decreases with distance for a positive charge
  • D) V = kq/r²
C is correct. V = kq/r — potential decreases with distance for a positive charge
Q14
Work done to move charge q through a potential difference ΔV is
  • A) W = qΔV
  • B) W = q/ΔV
  • C) W = qΔV²
  • D) W = ΔV/q
A is correct. W = qΔV
Q15
The magnetic field at the center of a circular loop of radius R carrying current I is
  • A) B = I/(2μ₀R)
  • B) B = μ₀I/(2R) — proportional to current, inversely proportional to radius
  • C) B = μ₀I/(2πR)
  • D) B = μ₀IR
B is correct. B = μ₀I/(2R) — proportional to current, inversely proportional to radius
Q16
Ohm's Law in terms of resistivity ρ, length L, and cross-section A gives resistance as
  • A) R = ρA/L
  • B) R = L/(ρA)
  • C) R = ρ/(LA)
  • D) R = ρL/A

  • A) a factor of κ: C = κε₀A/d (the dielectric reduces E, allowing more charge for same voltage)
  • B) decreasing the plate separation by κ
  • C) increasing the permittivity to ε₀/κ
  • D) reducing the stored energy by κ
A is correct. a factor of κ: C = κε₀A/d (the dielectric reduces E, allowing more charge for same voltage)
Q18
Electromagnetic waves are transverse waves in which E and B fields are
  • A) perpendicular to each other but parallel to propagation
  • B) in phase with the current source
  • C) perpendicular to each other and to the direction of wave propagation
  • D) parallel to each other and to the direction of propagation

Q19

Q20
