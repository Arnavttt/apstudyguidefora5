Q1
Newton's second law in rotational form states that the net torque equals
  • A) τ = rF (without moment of inertia)
  • B) τ_net = Iα — the product of moment of inertia and angular acceleration
  • C) τ = mv²/r
  • D) τ = Iω²
B is correct. τ_net = Iα — the product of moment of inertia and angular acceleration
Q2
The moment of inertia of a system of point masses about an axis is
  • A) I = Σmᵢ/rᵢ²
  • B) I = total mass × radius of gyration / 2
  • C) I = Σmᵢrᵢ² — the sum of each mass times its distance squared from the axis
  • D) I = mr (linear, not squared)
C is correct. I = Σmᵢrᵢ² — the sum of each mass times its distance squared from the axis
Q3
The parallel axis theorem states that
  • A) I_cm = I + Md²
  • B) I = I_cm + Md² — the moment of inertia about any axis equals I_cm plus mass times distance squared
  • C) I = I_cm × d²
  • D) I = I_cm / Md²
B is correct. I = I_cm + Md² — the moment of inertia about any axis equals I_cm plus mass times distance squared
Q4
Angular momentum L of a rigid body is conserved when
  • A) kinetic energy is conserved
  • B) the net external torque on the system is zero
  • C) the net external force is zero
  • D) the object is in static equilibrium
B is correct. the net external torque on the system is zero
Q5
The work-energy theorem for rotation states that
  • A) W = Iω (not squared)
  • B) W = Fv (translational only)
  • C) W_net = ΔKE_rot = Δ(½Iω²) — net rotational work equals change in rotational kinetic energy
  • D) W = τ × t (torque times time)
C is correct. W_net = ΔKE_rot = Δ(½Iω²) — net rotational work equals change in rotational kinetic energy
Q6
Universal gravitation states F = Gm₁m₂/r², where F is
  • A) proportional to distance (not inverse square)
  • B) attractive, directed along the line connecting the two masses
  • C) repulsive for like-sign masses
  • D) attractive only for spherical objects
B is correct. attractive, directed along the line connecting the two masses
Q7
Escape velocity is the minimum speed needed to
  • A) leave a gravitational field without further propulsion: v_esc = √(2GM/R)
  • B) achieve circular orbit at height R
  • C) escape Earth's atmosphere only
  • D) travel from one planet to another
A is correct. leave a gravitational field without further propulsion: v_esc = √(2GM/R)
Q8
Kepler's third law states that the square of the orbital period is
  • A) proportional to the square of the radius only
  • B) equal to the orbital speed squared
  • C) proportional to the cube of the semi-major axis: T² ∝ a³
  • D) inversely proportional to orbital speed
C is correct. proportional to the cube of the semi-major axis: T² ∝ a³
Q9
Simple harmonic motion (SHM) is characterized by a restoring force that is
  • A) proportional to and opposite in sign to the displacement from equilibrium
  • B) constant regardless of displacement
  • C) proportional to velocity
  • D) always directed upward
A is correct. proportional to and opposite in sign to the displacement from equilibrium
Q10
The angular frequency ω of a mass-spring system is
  • A) ω = mg/k
  • B) ω = √(k/m) — independent of amplitude
  • C) ω = √(m/k)
  • D) ω = 2πf/m
B is correct. ω = √(k/m) — independent of amplitude
Q11
In a two-body collision, conservation of linear momentum requires
  • A) the total momentum before equals total momentum after: Σp_before = Σp_after
  • B) kinetic energy is conserved in all collisions
  • C) the objects must stick together
  • D) the net external force may be nonzero
A is correct. the total momentum before equals total momentum after: Σp_before = Σp_after
Q12
A perfectly inelastic collision is one in which
  • A) momentum is not conserved
  • B) the colliding objects stick together and move as one; kinetic energy is not conserved
  • C) kinetic energy is fully conserved
  • D) the objects bounce apart elastically
B is correct. the colliding objects stick together and move as one; kinetic energy is not conserved
Q13
Power is defined in mechanics as
  • A) P = F/v
  • B) P = mv²
  • C) P = dW/dt = F·v — the rate at which work is done
  • D) P = W/d (work divided by distance)
C is correct. P = dW/dt = F·v — the rate at which work is done
Q14
A conservative force is one where
  • A) the work done is always positive
  • B) the force acts over infinite distance
  • C) the work done is path-independent; potential energy can be defined (e.g., gravity, spring force)
  • D) the force is always directed downward
C is correct. the work done is path-independent; potential energy can be defined (e.g., gravity, spring force)
Q15
The center of mass of a system moves as if
  • A) all external forces act on the total mass concentrated at that point
  • B) internal forces are the only forces acting
  • C) the system has no angular momentum
  • D) no external forces act on the system
A is correct. all external forces act on the total mass concentrated at that point
Q16
In circular motion, the centripetal acceleration is directed
  • A) tangent to the circle
  • B) away from the center (centrifugal)
  • C) in the direction of increasing speed
  • D) toward the center of the circle with magnitude v²/r = ω²r
D is correct. toward the center of the circle with magnitude v²/r = ω²r
Q17
The impulse-momentum theorem states that
  • A) J = ΔP = F_avg × Δt — impulse equals the change in momentum
  • B) F = ma (Newton's second law only)
  • C) J = mv² − mv₀²
  • D) J = W/v (work divided by velocity)
A is correct. J = ΔP = F_avg × Δt — impulse equals the change in momentum
Q18
Rolling without slipping requires that the contact point's velocity is
  • A) twice the rotational velocity
  • B) equal to the translational plus rotational velocity
  • C) zero (v_contact = 0): the velocity of the center equals ωR
  • D) equal to the center of mass velocity
C is correct. zero (v_contact = 0): the velocity of the center equals ωR
Q19
In simple harmonic motion, when displacement is maximum, velocity is
  • A) zero, and when displacement is zero (equilibrium), velocity is maximum
  • B) also maximum
  • C) equal to ω times displacement
  • D) always constant
A is correct. zero, and when displacement is zero (equilibrium), velocity is maximum
Q20

  • A) U = mgh² (squared)
  • B) U = mgh (taking the surface as reference)
