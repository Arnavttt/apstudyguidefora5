AP® Psychology — 20 course-level questions0 / 20 correct
Q1
The central nervous system consists of
  • A) the brain and peripheral nerves
  • B) sensory and motor neurons only
  • C) the autonomic and somatic systems
  • D) the brain and spinal cord
D is correct. the brain and spinal cord
Q2
Which neurotransmitter is most closely associated with reward, motivation, and movement?
  • A) Dopamine
  • B) Serotonin
  • C) GABA
  • D) Acetylcholine
A is correct. Dopamine
Q3
In classical conditioning, the conditioned stimulus (CS) originally
  • A) always elicited fear
  • B) produced no relevant response (was neutral) before conditioning
  • C) automatically triggered the unconditioned response
  • D) was paired with the unconditioned stimulus only once
B is correct. produced no relevant response (was neutral) before conditioning
Q4
Operant conditioning differs from classical conditioning in that it involves
  • A) learning through consequences — reinforcement and punishment of voluntary behavior
  • B) involuntary reflex responses to stimuli
  • C) association of two stimuli
  • D) observational learning only
A is correct. learning through consequences — reinforcement and punishment of voluntary behavior
Q5
According to Piaget, object permanence typically develops during the
  • A) Concrete operational stage
  • B) Formal operational stage
  • C) Sensorimotor stage (birth to ~2 years)
  • D) Preoperational stage
C is correct. Sensorimotor stage (birth to ~2 years)
Q6
Which part of the brain is most important for forming new long-term memories?
  • A) The cerebellum
  • B) The prefrontal cortex
  • C) The hippocampus
  • D) The amygdala
C is correct. The hippocampus
Q7
The serial position effect shows that memory is best for
  • A) items in the middle of a list
  • B) randomly placed items
  • C) emotionally neutral items
  • D) items at the beginning (primacy) and end (recency) of a list
D is correct. items at the beginning (primacy) and end (recency) of a list
Q8
Maslow's hierarchy of needs places __________ as the highest level of human motivation.
  • A) Esteem needs
  • B) Love and belonging
  • C) Safety needs
  • D) Self-actualization
D is correct. Self-actualization
Q9
The James-Lange theory of emotion proposes that
  • A) emotions precede physiological arousal
  • B) cognitive labeling of arousal determines emotion
  • C) the thalamus triggers emotion before the cortex
  • D) we feel emotion because we notice our physiological responses to a stimulus
D is correct. we feel emotion because we notice our physiological responses to a stimulus
Q10
A major difference between a major depressive disorder and bipolar disorder is that bipolar disorder also includes
  • A) manic episodes characterized by elevated mood, grandiosity, and decreased need for sleep
  • B) more severe depressive episodes
  • C) psychotic hallucinations in all cases
  • D) anxiety as a core feature
A is correct. manic episodes characterized by elevated mood, grandiosity, and decreased need for sleep
Q11
The peripheral nervous system is divided into the
  • A) sympathetic and parasympathetic systems only
  • B) central and peripheral systems
  • C) sensory and motor cortices
  • D) somatic and autonomic nervous systems
D is correct. somatic and autonomic nervous systems
Q12
Absolute threshold is defined as
  • A) the smallest detectable change between two stimuli
  • B) the maximum intensity before pain
  • C) the point of sensory adaptation
  • D) the minimum stimulus intensity detected 50% of the time
D is correct. the minimum stimulus intensity detected 50% of the time
Q13
According to Freud, the __________ operates on the reality principle and mediates between id and superego.
  • A) Ego
  • B) Superego
  • C) Id
  • D) Unconscious
A is correct. Ego
Q14
Confirmation bias refers to the tendency to
  • A) search for and favor information that confirms pre-existing beliefs
  • B) overestimate the likelihood of vivid events
  • C) judge based on a starting value
  • D) attribute others' behavior to personality rather than situation
A is correct. search for and favor information that confirms pre-existing beliefs
Q15
Social facilitation occurs when
  • A) the presence of others improves performance on simple or well-practiced tasks
  • B) group pressure leads individuals to conform
  • C) bystanders fail to help because others are present
  • D) individuals perform worse in groups than alone
A is correct. the presence of others improves performance on simple or well-practiced tasks
Q16
In a double-blind experiment, neither the __________ nor the __________ know who received the treatment.
  • A) participants nor the experimenters
  • B) control group nor the experimental group
  • C) researchers nor the IRB
  • D) hypothesis nor the method
A is correct. participants nor the experimenters
Q17
The heritability of a trait refers to
  • A) the proportion of variation in a trait within a population attributable to genetic differences
  • B) the percentage of the trait determined by genes in any individual
  • C) whether a trait is learned or innate
  • D) the chance that a trait is passed to offspring
A is correct. the proportion of variation in a trait within a population attributable to genetic differences
Q18
Which therapy is most closely associated with identifying and changing irrational thought patterns?
  • A) Psychoanalysis
  • B) Client-centered (humanistic) therapy
  • C) Systematic desensitization
  • D) Cognitive-behavioral therapy (CBT)
D is correct. Cognitive-behavioral therapy (CBT)
Q19
Bystander effect research (Latané & Darley) found that as group size increases,
  • A) conformity decreases
  • B) aggression increases
  • C) the likelihood of any individual helping decreases due to diffusion of responsibility
  • D) helping behavior increases due to social pressure
C is correct. the likelihood of any individual helping decreases due to diffusion of responsibility
Q20
The sympathetic nervous system prepares the body for "fight or flight" by
  • A) increasing heart rate, dilating pupils, and diverting blood to muscles
  • B) slowing heart rate and promoting digestion
  • C) releasing serotonin to reduce stress
  • D) activating the parasympathetic system simultaneously
A is correct. increasing heart rate, dilating pupils, and diverting blood to muscles
