Q1
The principle that government derives authority from the consent of the people is called
  • A) judicial review
  • B) separation of powers
  • C) popular sovereignty
  • D) federalism
C is correct. popular sovereignty
Q2
Judicial review — the power to strike down unconstitutional laws — was established by
  • A) Article III of the Constitution
  • B) the 5th Amendment
  • C) Marbury v. Madison (1803)
  • D) McCulloch v. Maryland (1819)
C is correct. Marbury v. Madison (1803)
Q3
Which constitutional clause makes federal law supreme over conflicting state laws?
  • A) The Necessary and Proper Clause
  • B) The 10th Amendment
  • C) The Supremacy Clause (Article VI)
  • D) The Commerce Clause
C is correct. The Supremacy Clause (Article VI)
Q4
The Necessary and Proper Clause grants Congress the power to
  • A) pass laws required to carry out its enumerated powers (implied powers)
  • B) override presidential vetoes
  • C) declare war unilaterally
  • D) appoint Supreme Court justices
A is correct. pass laws required to carry out its enumerated powers (implied powers)
Q5
An unfunded mandate requires states to
  • A) comply with a federal requirement without receiving adequate federal funding to cover the cost
  • B) spend block-grant money as the federal government directs
  • C) create new taxes
  • D) submit budget plans to Congress
A is correct. comply with a federal requirement without receiving adequate federal funding to cover the cost
Q6
Which chamber of Congress has the unique power to originate revenue (tax) bills?
  • A) The Senate
  • B) The Treasury Department
  • C) Either chamber equally
  • D) The House of Representatives
D is correct. The House of Representatives
Q7
A cloture vote in the Senate requires how many votes to end a filibuster?
  • A) 60 votes (three-fifths of the Senate)
  • B) 51 votes (simple majority)
  • C) 67 votes (two-thirds)
  • D) 75 votes (three-fourths)
A is correct. 60 votes (three-fifths of the Senate)
Q8
The War Powers Resolution (1973) requires the president to
  • A) allow Congress to appoint military commanders
  • B) notify Congress within 48 hours of deploying troops and withdraw within 60 days without congressional authorization
  • C) seek a formal declaration of war before any deployment
  • D) get Senate approval for all deployments
B is correct. notify Congress within 48 hours of deploying troops and withdraw within 60 days without congressional authorization
Q9
Executive orders are significant because they allow the president to
  • A) amend the Constitution unilaterally
  • B) override Supreme Court rulings
  • C) declare national emergencies requiring new taxes
  • D) direct federal agencies and policy without congressional legislation
D is correct. direct federal agencies and policy without congressional legislation
Q10
Iron triangles (issue networks) in U.S. policy-making involve
  • A) state governments, businesses, and foreign governments
  • B) congressional committees, executive agencies, and interest groups working together to shape policy
  • C) the president, Senate, and House only
  • D) federal courts, the media, and the public
B is correct. congressional committees, executive agencies, and interest groups working together to shape policy
Q11
Linkage institutions in American politics include
  • A) Congress, the presidency, and the courts
  • B) the bureaucracy and executive agencies
  • C) the Constitution and Bill of Rights
  • D) political parties, interest groups, elections, and the media
D is correct. political parties, interest groups, elections, and the media
Q12
The exclusionary rule (4th Amendment) holds that
  • A) suspects must be read Miranda rights before questioning
  • B) all confessions are inadmissible without a lawyer
  • C) police need no warrant to search public areas
  • D) evidence obtained through illegal searches cannot be used in court
D is correct. evidence obtained through illegal searches cannot be used in court
Q13
Tinker v. Des Moines (1969) established that
  • A) public schools may require the Pledge of Allegiance
  • B) students do not shed their constitutional rights at the schoolhouse gate (symbolic speech is protected)
  • C) schools may limit all student speech on campus
  • D) dress codes violate the 1st Amendment
B is correct. students do not shed their constitutional rights at the schoolhouse gate (symbolic speech is protected)
Q14
The elastic clause (Necessary and Proper Clause) is found in
  • A) The 10th Amendment
  • B) Article I, Section 8 of the Constitution
  • C) Article II
  • D) Article III
B is correct. Article I, Section 8 of the Constitution
Q15
Which of the following best describes categorical grants?
  • A) Revenue sharing with no strings attached
  • B) Loans that states must repay
  • C) Federal funds given to states for specific, narrowly defined purposes with strict conditions
  • D) Funds states may spend freely within broad policy areas
C is correct. Federal funds given to states for specific, narrowly defined purposes with strict conditions
Q16
The incorporation doctrine applies the Bill of Rights to states through the
  • A) 14th Amendment's Due Process Clause
  • B) 10th Amendment
  • C) Supremacy Clause
  • D) Commerce Clause
A is correct. 14th Amendment's Due Process Clause
Q17
McCulloch v. Maryland (1819) ruled that
  • A) the Commerce Clause allows Congress to regulate interstate commerce broadly
  • B) Congress has broad implied powers and states cannot tax federal institutions
  • C) the Bill of Rights applies to states
  • D) judicial review is constitutional
B is correct. Congress has broad implied powers and states cannot tax federal institutions
Q18
The Senate's advice and consent role applies to
  • A) all executive orders
  • B) federal budget appropriations
  • C) declarations of war
  • D) presidential nominations for federal judges and ambassadors, and treaty ratification
D is correct. presidential nominations for federal judges and ambassadors, and treaty ratification
Q19
Devolution refers to
  • A) expanding federal power over states
  • B) the process of constitutional amendment
  • C) eliminating state governments
  • D) the transfer of policy-making authority from the federal government to state governments
D is correct. the transfer of policy-making authority from the federal government to state governments
Q20
The First Amendment protects freedom of speech, religion, press, petition, and
  • A) privacy
  • B) bearing arms
  • C) due process
  • D) assembly (the right to gather peacefully)
D is correct. assembly (the right to gather peacefully)
