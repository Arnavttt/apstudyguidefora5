Q1
The primary goal of the Columbian Exchange was
  • A) the widespread transfer of plants, animals, culture, and diseases between the Americas and the Old World after 1492
  • B) establishing European colonies in Asia
  • C) creating a trade route to China
  • D) converting indigenous peoples to Christianity only
A is correct. the widespread transfer of plants, animals, culture, and diseases between the Americas and the Old World after 1492
Q2
The Enlightenment idea most reflected in the Declaration of Independence is
  • A) divine right of kings
  • B) manifest destiny
  • C) natural rights — that all people possess inherent rights to life, liberty, and property
  • D) mercantilism
C is correct. natural rights — that all people possess inherent rights to life, liberty, and property
Q3
The Missouri Compromise of 1820 primarily addressed
  • A) the sectional conflict over slavery by admitting Missouri as a slave state and Maine as a free state, and banning slavery north of 36°30'
  • B) the disputed election of 1824
  • C) the War of 1812 peace terms
  • D) tariff policy between North and South
A is correct. the sectional conflict over slavery by admitting Missouri as a slave state and Maine as a free state, and banning slavery north of 36°30'
Q4
Manifest Destiny as an ideology justified
  • A) protective tariffs on manufactured goods
  • B) U.S. westward territorial expansion as divinely ordained and racially inevitable
  • C) industrialization of the Northeast
  • D) neutrality in European conflicts
B is correct. U.S. westward territorial expansion as divinely ordained and racially inevitable
Q5
The Compromise of 1850 was significant because it
  • A) permanently settled the Kansas slavery question
  • B) established the transcontinental railroad
  • C) created the Republican Party
  • D) admitted California as a free state, organized other Mexican Cession territories by popular sovereignty, and included a stronger Fugitive Slave Act
D is correct. admitted California as a free state, organized other Mexican Cession territories by popular sovereignty, and included a stronger Fugitive Slave Act
Q6
Reconstruction (1865–1877) primarily attempted to
  • A) redistribute Southern plantation land to freedpeople permanently
  • B) rapidly industrialize the South
  • C) restore the seceded states to the Union while defining and protecting the rights of freedpeople
  • D) punish Confederate soldiers with prison terms
C is correct. restore the seceded states to the Union while defining and protecting the rights of freedpeople
Q7
The Gilded Age (1870s–1900) was characterized by
  • A) rapid industrialization, massive wealth inequality, political corruption, and the rise of corporate monopolies
  • B) broad reform movements addressing social inequality
  • C) declining immigration to the United States
  • D) significant gains for organized labor
A is correct. rapid industrialization, massive wealth inequality, political corruption, and the rise of corporate monopolies
Q8
The Progressive Era reform movement sought to
  • A) reduce immigration permanently
  • B) address industrial capitalism's abuses through government regulation, democracy expansion, and social welfare programs
  • C) return the U.S. to an agrarian economy
  • D) expand U.S. colonial empire
B is correct. address industrial capitalism's abuses through government regulation, democracy expansion, and social welfare programs
Q9
The U.S. entered World War I in 1917 primarily because
  • A) German unrestricted submarine warfare threatened American lives and commerce, and the Zimmermann Telegram revealed German overtures to Mexico
  • B) the U.S. sought to acquire European colonies
  • C) Britain demanded U.S. military intervention
  • D) Congress declared war on Austria-Hungary first
A is correct. German unrestricted submarine warfare threatened American lives and commerce, and the Zimmermann Telegram revealed German overtures to Mexico
Q10
The New Deal programs of the 1930s aimed to
  • A) end international trade to protect U.S. workers
  • B) provide relief, recovery, and reform in response to the Great Depression through federal intervention in the economy
  • C) permanently expand U.S. military spending
  • D) reduce the national debt immediately
B is correct. provide relief, recovery, and reform in response to the Great Depression through federal intervention in the economy
Q11
The Korean War (1950–1953) ended in
  • A) an armistice restoring roughly the pre-war boundary at the 38th parallel with Korea remaining divided
  • B) a U.S. military victory and unified democratic Korea
  • C) a North Korean victory and occupation of the South
  • D) UN occupation of the entire peninsula
A is correct. an armistice restoring roughly the pre-war boundary at the 38th parallel with Korea remaining divided
Q12
The GI Bill (Servicemen's Readjustment Act, 1944) contributed to postwar prosperity by
  • A) creating the Interstate Highway System
  • B) establishing Medicare and Medicaid
  • C) ending racial segregation in the military
  • D) providing veterans with education, housing loans, and unemployment benefits, fueling suburban growth and a college-educated middle class
D is correct. providing veterans with education, housing loans, and unemployment benefits, fueling suburban growth and a college-educated middle class
Q13
Brown v. Board of Education (1954) overturned which earlier Supreme Court precedent?
  • A) Marbury v. Madison (1803)
  • B) Korematsu v. United States (1944)
  • C) Plessy v. Ferguson (1896), which established the "separate but equal" doctrine
  • D) Dred Scott v. Sandford (1857)
C is correct. Plessy v. Ferguson (1896), which established the "separate but equal" doctrine
Q14
The domino theory used to justify U.S. involvement in Vietnam argued that
  • A) the Soviet Union directly controlled North Vietnam
  • B) if one country fell to communism, neighboring countries would follow in a chain reaction
  • C) the U.S. needed Southeast Asian oil reserves
  • D) Vietnam was strategically essential for Pacific trade
B is correct. if one country fell to communism, neighboring countries would follow in a chain reaction
Q15
The Great Society programs of LBJ (1960s) included
  • A) the New Deal reforms extended to new groups
  • B) the creation of Social Security
  • C) the interstate highway system and NASA
  • D) Medicare, Medicaid, the Civil Rights Act, the Voting Rights Act, and expanded federal education funding
D is correct. Medicare, Medicaid, the Civil Rights Act, the Voting Rights Act, and expanded federal education funding
Q16
The Three-Fifths Compromise at the Constitutional Convention determined that
  • A) slavery would be abolished by 1808
  • B) slave states would receive equal Senate representation
  • C) the slave trade would be taxed
  • D) enslaved people would count as three-fifths of a person for apportionment of congressional seats and direct taxes
D is correct. enslaved people would count as three-fifths of a person for apportionment of congressional seats and direct taxes
Q17
The Homestead Act of 1862 stimulated western settlement by
  • A) requiring settlers to purchase land at auction
  • B) offering 160 acres of public land to settlers who farmed it for five years
  • C) guaranteeing military protection from Native American tribes
  • D) subsidizing railroad construction westward
B is correct. offering 160 acres of public land to settlers who farmed it for five years
Q18
The Women's Suffrage movement achieved its primary goal with
  • A) the Civil Rights Act (1964)
  • B) the 19th Amendment (1920), which prohibited denying the right to vote on the basis of sex
  • C) the 15th Amendment (1870)
  • D) the Equal Rights Amendment (1972)
B is correct. the 19th Amendment (1920), which prohibited denying the right to vote on the basis of sex
Q19

  • D) Hirabayashi v. United States (1943)

Q20

  • A) discrimination in housing only
  • B) race, color, religion, sex, or national origin in employment and public accommodations
