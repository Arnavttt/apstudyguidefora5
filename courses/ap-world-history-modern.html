Q1
The Silk Roads were significant primarily because they
  • A) facilitated exchange of goods, ideas, religions, and diseases across Afro-Eurasia from c. 200 BCE to 1450 CE
  • B) were used exclusively for silk trade between China and Rome
  • C) connected sub-Saharan Africa to Europe for the first time
  • D) replaced Indian Ocean trade by 1000 CE
A is correct. facilitated exchange of goods, ideas, religions, and diseases across Afro-Eurasia from c. 200 BCE to 1450 CE
Q2
The Mongol Empire's most lasting impact on Eurasian history was
  • A) creating the "Pax Mongolica" that facilitated trade, communication, and disease transmission (Black Death) across the continent
  • B) spreading Islam throughout Europe
  • C) permanently destroying Chinese civilization
  • D) establishing the Ottoman Empire
A is correct. creating the "Pax Mongolica" that facilitated trade, communication, and disease transmission (Black Death) across the continent
Q3
The Indian Ocean trade network (c. 600–1450) was primarily driven by
  • A) monsoon winds that enabled seasonal sailing, connecting East Africa, Arabia, India, and Southeast Asia
  • B) European maritime technology
  • C) overland routes through Central Asia
  • D) direct Chinese state control of all shipping
A is correct. monsoon winds that enabled seasonal sailing, connecting East Africa, Arabia, India, and Southeast Asia
Q4
The Protestant Reformation challenged the Catholic Church primarily by
  • A) advocating for complete separation of church and state
  • B) promoting polytheism across Europe
  • C) creating Islamic reform movements
  • D) rejecting papal authority, indulgences, and the doctrine of salvation through works, emphasizing scripture and faith alone
D is correct. rejecting papal authority, indulgences, and the doctrine of salvation through works, emphasizing scripture and faith alone
Q5
The Atlantic slave trade transformed West Africa by
  • A) depopulating regions, causing political instability, and making African rulers dependent on European trade goods for slave-raiding weapons
  • B) immediately industrializing coastal West African societies
  • C) unifying sub-Saharan Africa under one government
  • D) introducing Christianity to all coastal populations
A is correct. depopulating regions, causing political instability, and making African rulers dependent on European trade goods for slave-raiding weapons
Q6
The Colombian Exchange's most devastating effect on indigenous American populations was
  • A) military conquest by European armies alone
  • B) forced conversion to Christianity
  • C) introduction of plantation agriculture
  • D) the introduction of Eurasian diseases (smallpox, measles) that killed an estimated 50–90% of native populations
D is correct. the introduction of Eurasian diseases (smallpox, measles) that killed an estimated 50–90% of native populations
Q7
Mercantilism as an economic policy promoted
  • A) free trade and open markets
  • B) abolishing colonial relationships
  • C) equal trade between European powers
  • D) accumulating national wealth through favorable trade balances, controlling colonies for raw materials and captive markets
D is correct. accumulating national wealth through favorable trade balances, controlling colonies for raw materials and captive markets
Q8
The French Revolution (1789) was primarily caused by
  • A) the collapse of the Atlantic slave trade
  • B) fiscal crisis, Enlightenment ideals challenging royal authority, and social inequality between the estates
  • C) British military invasion
  • D) religious conflict between Catholics and Protestants
B is correct. fiscal crisis, Enlightenment ideals challenging royal authority, and social inequality between the estates
Q9
Industrialization first began in Britain primarily because of
  • A) government-directed planning of the economy
  • B) technological innovations imported from China
  • C) the absence of serfdom throughout British history
  • D) abundant coal and iron deposits, capital from trade and empire, a market-friendly legal system, and early agricultural enclosure that freed labor
D is correct. abundant coal and iron deposits, capital from trade and empire, a market-friendly legal system, and early agricultural enclosure that freed labor
Q10
Nationalism in 19th-century Europe primarily led to
  • A) peaceful cooperation between all European states
  • B) the abolition of monarchy throughout Europe
  • C) the formation of the League of Nations
  • D) the unification of Germany and Italy and the breakup of multi-ethnic empires (Ottoman, Habsburg)
D is correct. the unification of Germany and Italy and the breakup of multi-ethnic empires (Ottoman, Habsburg)
Q11
The Berlin Conference of 1884–85 established rules for
  • A) European powers to partition Africa among themselves without African input (the "Scramble for Africa")
  • B) ending the transatlantic slave trade
  • C) establishing the first pan-African government
  • D) regulating European colonialism in Asia
A is correct. European powers to partition Africa among themselves without African input (the "Scramble for Africa")
Q12
World War I was triggered by the assassination of Archduke Franz Ferdinand, but underlying causes included
  • A) the collapse of the British Empire
  • B) U.S. entry into European politics
  • C) religious conflict between Christian and Muslim nations
  • D) militarism, entangling alliances, imperialism, and nationalism (MAIN)
D is correct. militarism, entangling alliances, imperialism, and nationalism (MAIN)
Q13
The Russian Revolution of 1917 resulted in
  • A) Russia joining the Allied Powers more fully in WWI
  • B) the creation of a democratic republic
  • C) the overthrow of the Tsar and eventually the Bolshevik seizure of power, creating the world's first communist state
  • D) a constitutional monarchy modeled on Britain
C is correct. the overthrow of the Tsar and eventually the Bolshevik seizure of power, creating the world's first communist state
Q14
The Great Depression spread globally after 1929 primarily because of
  • A) overproduction of oil in the Middle East
  • B) the interconnectedness of the international financial system, collapsing trade, and bank failures across countries
  • C) a worldwide crop failure
  • D) the end of WWI reparations payments
B is correct. the interconnectedness of the international financial system, collapsing trade, and bank failures across countries
Q15
Decolonization after WWII was accelerated by
  • A) weakened European powers, nationalist movements, Cold War ideological competition, and UN support for self-determination
  • B) unanimous agreement among colonial powers to grant independence
  • C) U.S. military liberation of all colonies
  • D) the collapse of the League of Nations
A is correct. weakened European powers, nationalist movements, Cold War ideological competition, and UN support for self-determination
Q16
Mao Zedong's Cultural Revolution (1966–76) aimed to
  • A) create a market economy
  • B) purge capitalist and traditional elements from Chinese society and reassert Mao's political dominance through mass mobilization of youth
  • C) industrialize China rapidly using Soviet technology
  • D) open China to foreign investment
B is correct. purge capitalist and traditional elements from Chinese society and reassert Mao's political dominance through mass mobilization of youth
Q17
The Green Revolution of the mid-20th century increased food production in developing nations through
  • A) high-yield crop varieties, chemical fertilizers, pesticides, and irrigation, particularly in South Asia
  • B) redistribution of land to peasant farmers
  • C) elimination of traditional farming entirely
  • D) reducing population growth rates
A is correct. high-yield crop varieties, chemical fertilizers, pesticides, and irrigation, particularly in South Asia
Q18
The Cold War was primarily characterized by
  • A) ideological, political, and economic competition between the U.S.-led capitalist West and Soviet-led communist East without direct military conflict between superpowers
  • B) constant conventional warfare between the U.S. and USSR
  • C) economic cooperation between superpowers
  • D) nuclear war that was quickly contained
A is correct. ideological, political, and economic competition between the U.S.-led capitalist West and Soviet-led communist East without direct military conflict between superpowers
Q19
Globalization since 1980 has been driven primarily by
  • A) advances in communication and transportation technology, trade liberalization, and the spread of multinational corporations
  • B) government-to-government aid programs
  • C) decline of manufacturing in developing nations
  • D) deliberate policies to reduce international migration
A is correct. advances in communication and transportation technology, trade liberalization, and the spread of multinational corporations
Q20
The Human Development Index (HDI) measures national development using
  • A) carbon emissions and industrial output
  • B) life expectancy, education (years of schooling), and gross national income per capita
  • C) GDP growth rate and military spending
  • D) population density and urbanization rate
B is correct. life expectancy, education (years of schooling), and gross national income per capita
