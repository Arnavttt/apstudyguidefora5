  • A) 270°, calculated as (3π/2) × (180°/π) = 270°
  • B) 90°
  • C) 180°
  • D) 360°
A is correct. 270°, calculated as (3π/2) × (180°/π) = 270°
Q2
The unit circle is defined as
  • A) a circle of radius 1 centered at the origin; coordinates on it are (cos θ, sin θ)
  • B) a circle of radius π centered at the origin
  • C) a circle centered at (1, 0) with radius 1
  • D) a semicircle with diameter 1
A is correct. a circle of radius 1 centered at the origin; coordinates on it are (cos θ, sin θ)
Q3
In a right triangle, the sine of an acute angle θ equals
  • A) adjacent/hypotenuse
  • B) opposite/adjacent
  • C) hypotenuse/opposite
  • D) opposite/hypotenuse
D is correct. opposite/hypotenuse
Q4
The cosine function cos(θ) is an even function because
  • A) it outputs only positive values
  • B) it has period π
  • C) cos(θ) = 1/sin(θ)
  • D) cos(-θ) = cos(θ) — its graph is symmetric about the y-axis
D is correct. cos(-θ) = cos(θ) — its graph is symmetric about the y-axis
Q5
The period of f(x) = sin(2x) is
  • A) 2
  • B) π — the period is 2π divided by the coefficient of x: 2π/2 = π

  • D) 4π
B is correct. π — the period is 2π divided by the coefficient of x: 2π/2 = π
Q6

  • D) tan^2(θ)
B is correct. 1 — this is true for every angle θ and follows directly from the unit circle
Q7
