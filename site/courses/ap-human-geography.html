Q1
The difference between absolute location and relative location is that absolute location
  • A) uses a fixed coordinate system (latitude/longitude) while relative location describes position in relation to other places
  • B) is always more useful for navigation
  • C) changes over time while relative location is fixed
  • D) only applies to cities, not regions
A is correct. uses a fixed coordinate system (latitude/longitude) while relative location describes position in relation to other places
Q2
Ravenstein's Laws of Migration suggest that most migrants
  • A) are young men moving from cities to rural areas
  • B) move internationally more often than internally
  • C) are motivated primarily by political push factors
  • D) move short distances and toward urban areas (step migration), with long-distance moves being less common
D is correct. move short distances and toward urban areas (step migration), with long-distance moves being less common
Q3
The demographic transition model's Stage 3 is characterized by
  • A) very low birth rates and aging population
  • B) population growth driven solely by immigration
  • C) declining birth rates as economic development, urbanization, and women's education increase
  • D) high birth and death rates that are roughly equal
C is correct. declining birth rates as economic development, urbanization, and women's education increase
Q4
Gerrymandering refers to
  • A) redrawing maps based on census data alone
  • B) the process of granting statehood to territories
  • C) manipulating electoral district boundaries to favor a particular political party or group
  • D) the natural migration of populations across borders
C is correct. manipulating electoral district boundaries to favor a particular political party or group
Q5
The concept of "sense of place" in human geography refers to
  • A) the physical characteristics of a landscape
  • B) the subjective, emotional meaning people attach to locations based on personal and cultural experiences
  • C) the exact coordinates of a specific location
  • D) the administrative boundaries of a place
B is correct. the subjective, emotional meaning people attach to locations based on personal and cultural experiences
Q6
Globalization has generally led to
  • A) greater cultural homogenization and economic interdependence, but also local resistance (glocalization)
  • B) complete elimination of cultural differences worldwide
  • C) reduced migration between countries
  • D) less international trade and investment
A is correct. greater cultural homogenization and economic interdependence, but also local resistance (glocalization)
Q7
The gravity model in human geography predicts that
  • A) interaction between two places increases with their population size and decreases with distance between them
  • B) cities grow proportionally to their rural surroundings
  • C) migration is always from rural to urban areas
  • D) trade flows are determined only by cultural similarity
A is correct. interaction between two places increases with their population size and decreases with distance between them
Q8
Forced migration differs from voluntary migration in that forced migration
  • A) results in permanent settlement in all cases
  • B) occurs when individuals are compelled to leave by threats, persecution, or disaster (not free choice)
  • C) only applies to international movement, not internal
  • D) is driven by economic opportunity
B is correct. occurs when individuals are compelled to leave by threats, persecution, or disaster (not free choice)
Q9
Bid-rent theory explains why
  • A) cities always develop in concentric zones
  • B) suburban areas have higher rents than downtowns
  • C) agricultural land is always found at city edges
  • D) land use intensity and rent decrease with distance from the urban center (CBD)
D is correct. land use intensity and rent decrease with distance from the urban center (CBD)
Q10
Ethnic enclaves in cities form primarily because
  • A) enclaves always form near industrial zones
  • B) ethnic groups are legally required to live together
  • C) immigrants cluster for cultural support, economic networks, and shared services in familiar communities
  • D) governments designate specific zones for ethnic groups
C is correct. immigrants cluster for cultural support, economic networks, and shared services in familiar communities
Q11
The von Thünen model of agricultural land use predicts that
  • A) agricultural specialization is determined by climate alone
  • B) transportation costs have no effect on crop location
  • C) all farms grow the same crops regardless of market distance
  • D) land use is arranged in rings around a market city, with intensive production nearest and extensive production farthest away
D is correct. land use is arranged in rings around a market city, with intensive production nearest and extensive production farthest away
Q12
Centripetal forces in a state are forces that
  • A) push populations toward urban centers
  • B) create demographic decline in rural areas
  • C) unify and strengthen national cohesion and identity
  • D) pull regions away from the central government (devolution)
C is correct. unify and strengthen national cohesion and identity
Q13
The rank-size rule states that
  • A) all cities in a country have similar populations
  • B) the nth largest city in a country has 1/n the population of the largest city
  • C) cities grow in rank until they equal the capital
  • D) the largest city is always the capital
B is correct. the nth largest city in a country has 1/n the population of the largest city
Q14
Core-periphery theory in world-systems theory argues that
  • A) all nations develop economically at the same rate
  • B) peripheral nations produce high-value manufactured goods
  • C) geography alone determines economic development
  • D) wealthy core nations exploit peripheral nations for cheap labor and resources, reinforcing global inequality
D is correct. wealthy core nations exploit peripheral nations for cheap labor and resources, reinforcing global inequality
Q15
Cultural diffusion occurs when
  • A) ideas, practices, or innovations spread from one culture to another through contact
  • B) cultures remain isolated from one another
  • C) a culture adopts all traits of a neighboring group
  • D) new technologies are independently invented in multiple places
A is correct. ideas, practices, or innovations spread from one culture to another through contact
Q16
Urbanization in the developing world is primarily driven by
  • A) government mandates requiring city residence
  • B) rural-to-urban migration due to push factors (poverty, lack of jobs) and urban pull factors (employment, services)
  • C) natural population increase within cities only
  • D) foreign direct investment eliminating rural areas
B is correct. rural-to-urban migration due to push factors (poverty, lack of jobs) and urban pull factors (employment, services)
Q17
A primate city is characterized by
  • A) having the highest quality of life in the country
  • B) being equidistant from all other major cities
  • C) having a population exactly twice the second city
  • D) being disproportionately large compared to the next-largest cities in the country, dominating politics and culture
D is correct. being disproportionately large compared to the next-largest cities in the country, dominating politics and culture
Q18
The concept of "time-space compression" refers to
  • A) the physical shrinking of urban areas due to automation
  • B) the decline of rural areas over time
  • C) the standardization of time zones globally
  • D) the perceived shrinking of the world due to technologies that reduce the time needed to communicate and travel
D is correct. the perceived shrinking of the world due to technologies that reduce the time needed to communicate and travel
Q19
The Green Revolution of the mid-20th century increased food production primarily through
  • A) expansion of farmland into rainforests
  • B) eliminating all traditional farming practices globally
  • C) reducing population growth in agricultural countries
  • D) new high-yield crop varieties, chemical fertilizers, pesticides, and irrigation technology
D is correct. new high-yield crop varieties, chemical fertilizers, pesticides, and irrigation technology
Q20
Supranationalism refers to
  • A) political and economic cooperation among nations that transfers some sovereignty to a shared authority (e.g., EU)
  • B) a single nation's dominance over others militarily
  • C) the breakup of multi-ethnic states into smaller nations
  • D) cultural imperialism by powerful nations
A is correct. political and economic cooperation among nations that transfers some sovereignty to a shared authority (e.g., EU)
