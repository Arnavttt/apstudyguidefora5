AP® Microeconomics — 20 course-level questions0 / 20 correct
Q1
The law of demand states that, all else equal, as price increases, quantity demanded
  • A) increases
  • B) remains constant
  • C) increases then decreases
  • D) decreases (inverse relationship)
D is correct. decreases (inverse relationship)
Q2
A perfectly competitive firm is a price taker because
  • A) it faces a downward-sloping demand curve
  • B) government regulations fix the price
  • C) it produces a differentiated product
  • D) its output is so small relative to the market that it cannot influence the market price
D is correct. its output is so small relative to the market that it cannot influence the market price
Q3
For a profit-maximizing firm, the optimal output level is where
  • A) average total cost (ATC) is minimized
  • B) total revenue is maximized
  • C) average revenue exceeds average cost by the most
  • D) marginal revenue (MR) equals marginal cost (MC)
D is correct. marginal revenue (MR) equals marginal cost (MC)
Q4
A monopolist maximizes profit by producing where MR = MC, but charges a price
  • A) equal to MC to maximize efficiency
  • B) equal to average variable cost
  • C) below MC to increase market share
  • D) on the demand curve above MC, capturing consumer surplus as producer surplus
D is correct. on the demand curve above MC, capturing consumer surplus as producer surplus
Q5
Price discrimination allows a monopolist to
  • A) lower prices for all consumers
  • B) eliminate deadweight loss entirely
  • C) set price equal to marginal cost
  • D) charge different prices to different consumers based on willingness to pay, increasing profit
D is correct. charge different prices to different consumers based on willingness to pay, increasing profit
Q6
The cross-price elasticity of demand between two substitute goods is
  • A) negative
  • B) zero
  • C) greater than one in absolute value
  • D) positive — when the price of one rises, demand for the substitute increases
D is correct. positive — when the price of one rises, demand for the substitute increases
Q7
Economies of scale occur when
  • A) average fixed cost rises with output
  • B) long-run average total cost decreases as output expands
  • C) short-run marginal cost falls below average variable cost
  • D) a firm enters a new market
B is correct. long-run average total cost decreases as output expands
Q8
A negative externality causes the market to produce
  • A) exactly the socially optimal quantity
  • B) zero output without government intervention
  • C) more than the socially optimal quantity, creating deadweight loss
  • D) less than the socially optimal quantity
C is correct. more than the socially optimal quantity, creating deadweight loss
Q9
A Pigouvian tax on a negative externality is set equal to
  • A) the market price
  • B) the firm's average total cost
  • C) the marginal private cost
  • D) the marginal external cost at the socially optimal quantity
D is correct. the marginal external cost at the socially optimal quantity
Q10
In a perfectly competitive market in long-run equilibrium, economic profit equals
  • A) a positive normal profit
  • B) the same as accounting profit
  • C) the deadweight loss avoided
  • D) zero (P = minimum ATC)
D is correct. zero (P = minimum ATC)
Q11
A monopsony has market power in
  • A) the labor market as the sole (or dominant) buyer of labor
  • B) the product market as the sole seller
  • C) the capital market
  • D) the government procurement market only
A is correct. the labor market as the sole (or dominant) buyer of labor
Q12
The marginal resource cost (MRC) for a monopsonist is
  • A) less than the wage due to market power
  • B) zero in the short run
  • C) greater than the wage because hiring one more worker requires paying all workers the higher wage
  • D) equal to the wage at every output level
C is correct. greater than the wage because hiring one more worker requires paying all workers the higher wage
Q13
In an oligopoly, the kinked demand curve model predicts
  • A) zero economic profit in all periods
  • B) price rigidity because rivals match price cuts but not price increases
  • C) frequent price changes due to competition
  • D) prices equal to marginal cost
B is correct. price rigidity because rivals match price cuts but not price increases
Q14
The substitution effect of a wage increase causes workers to
  • A) work fewer hours because they can maintain income with less work
  • B) exit the labor market
  • C) demand higher benefits instead
  • D) work more hours because leisure has become relatively more expensive
D is correct. work more hours because leisure has become relatively more expensive
Q15
Consumer surplus is defined as
  • A) the difference between what consumers are willing to pay and what they actually pay
  • B) total revenue minus total cost
  • C) producer profit above the market price
  • D) the area below the supply curve and above price
A is correct. the difference between what consumers are willing to pay and what they actually pay
Q16
Allocative efficiency is achieved when
  • A) average total cost is minimized
  • B) there is no economic profit
  • C) output is at the production possibilities frontier
  • D) resources are allocated so that P = MC (value to consumers equals cost of production)
D is correct. resources are allocated so that P = MC (value to consumers equals cost of production)
Q17
The income effect of a price decrease causes consumers to
  • A) shift demand for substitutes rightward
  • B) reduce quantity demanded
  • C) buy more because their real purchasing power has increased
  • D) buy less because the good is now "inferior"
C is correct. buy more because their real purchasing power has increased
Q18
A tax on a good with inelastic demand will
  • A) create a large deadweight loss
  • B) raise more revenue and impose most of the burden on consumers
  • C) reduce quantity demanded sharply
  • D) be shared equally between buyers and sellers
B is correct. raise more revenue and impose most of the burden on consumers
Q19
Natural monopolies arise when
  • A) products are perfectly differentiated
  • B) fixed costs are low relative to variable costs
  • C) a single firm can supply the entire market at lower cost than multiple competing firms (large economies of scale)
  • D) governments grant exclusive licenses
C is correct. a single firm can supply the entire market at lower cost than multiple competing firms (large economies of scale)
Q20
The concept of diminishing marginal returns applies in the
  • A) short run, when at least one input is fixed and adding more of the variable input eventually yields smaller output gains
  • B) long run when all inputs are variable
  • C) monopoly market structure
  • D) perfectly competitive long-run equilibrium
A is correct. short run, when at least one input is fixed and adding more of the variable input eventually yields smaller output gains
