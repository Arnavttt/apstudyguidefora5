Q1
Newton's second law states that the net force on an object equals
  • A) the product of its mass and acceleration: F_net = ma
  • B) the product of its weight and velocity
  • C) the change in momentum over time (impulse)
  • D) the sum of all individual forces acting on it (they are the same thing, but this answer is incomplete without the "net" part)
A is correct. the product of its mass and acceleration: F_net = ma
Q2
The work-energy theorem states that the net work done on an object equals
  • A) the product of force and mass
  • B) the change in potential energy
  • C) the change in the object's kinetic energy: W_net = ΔKE
  • D) the object's total mechanical energy
C is correct. the change in the object's kinetic energy: W_net = ΔKE
Q3
Conservation of momentum applies when
  • A) the net external force on the system is zero (closed, isolated system)
  • B) kinetic energy is conserved
  • C) the objects are in contact
  • D) gravitational potential energy is neglected
A is correct. the net external force on the system is zero (closed, isolated system)
Q4
A wave's frequency and period are related by
  • A) f = 1/T — frequency is the reciprocal of the period
  • B) f = T × wavelength
  • C) f = T/2 for mechanical waves
  • D) f = T × wave speed
A is correct. f = 1/T — frequency is the reciprocal of the period
Q5
In simple harmonic motion, the restoring force is
  • A) directed toward maximum displacement
  • B) proportional to and directed opposite to the displacement from equilibrium
  • C) constant regardless of displacement
  • D) proportional to velocity
B is correct. proportional to and directed opposite to the displacement from equilibrium
Q6
Torque is defined as
  • A) τ = mass × velocity × radius
  • B) τ = r × F sin θ — the product of the force, the lever arm, and the sine of the angle between them
  • C) τ = mass × angular acceleration only
  • D) τ = F/r (force divided by radius)
B is correct. τ = r × F sin θ — the product of the force, the lever arm, and the sine of the angle between them
Q7
Bernoulli's principle states that in a flowing fluid,
  • A) fluid speed is inversely proportional to density
  • B) as fluid speed increases, pressure decreases (energy conservation in fluid flow)
  • C) pressure is constant throughout any closed pipe
  • D) faster flow always creates more turbulence
B is correct. as fluid speed increases, pressure decreases (energy conservation in fluid flow)
Q8
Electric potential (voltage) is defined as
  • A) charge divided by capacitance
  • B) power divided by current
  • C) the electric potential energy per unit charge at a given point: V = U/q
  • D) the force per unit charge (that's electric field)
C is correct. the electric potential energy per unit charge at a given point: V = U/q
Q9
Ohm's Law states
  • A) P = IV (that's power)
  • B) R = V × I
  • C) I = V × R
  • D) V = IR — voltage equals current times resistance
D is correct. V = IR — voltage equals current times resistance
Q10
In a parallel circuit, the voltage across each branch is
  • A) zero for all but the branch with least resistance
  • B) equal to the voltage of the source (same across all branches)
  • C) divided equally among the branches
  • D) proportional to each branch's resistance
B is correct. equal to the voltage of the source (same across all branches)
Q11
The magnitude of the electric force between two point charges is given by
  • A) F = qE for charges at rest
  • B) Coulomb's Law: F = kq₁q₂/r², proportional to the product of charges and inversely proportional to distance squared
  • C) F = q₁q₂ × r²
  • D) F = kq/r (analogous to gravity with mass)
B is correct. Coulomb's Law: F = kq₁q₂/r², proportional to the product of charges and inversely proportional to distance squared
Q12
The direction of the magnetic force on a positive charge moving in a magnetic field is given by
  • A) always toward the north pole
  • B) the right-hand rule: F = qv × B (perpendicular to both v and B)
  • C) parallel to the magnetic field
  • D) opposite to the charge's velocity
B is correct. the right-hand rule: F = qv × B (perpendicular to both v and B)
Q13
Electromagnetic induction (Faraday's Law) states that an EMF is induced in a loop when
  • A) the magnetic flux through the loop changes over time: ε = −dΦ_B/dt
  • B) a current flows through the wire
  • C) the magnetic field is constant but strong
  • D) the loop moves at constant velocity in a uniform field
A is correct. the magnetic flux through the loop changes over time: ε = −dΦ_B/dt
Q14
The index of refraction n of a material is defined as
  • A) n = wavelength in vacuum / wavelength in medium
  • B) n = frequency × wavelength in medium
  • C) n = c/v — the ratio of the speed of light in vacuum to the speed in the material
  • D) n = v/c (speed in material divided by c)
C is correct. n = c/v — the ratio of the speed of light in vacuum to the speed in the material
Q15
In a converging (convex) lens, the focal length is
  • A) the distance from the object to the lens
  • B) the magnification times the object distance
  • C) negative for all converging lenses
  • D) the distance from the lens at which parallel rays converge to a focus
D is correct. the distance from the lens at which parallel rays converge to a focus
Q16
The first law of thermodynamics (energy conservation) states
  • A) heat flows from cold to hot with added work
  • B) total energy = kinetic + potential at all times
  • C) ΔU = Q − W: the change in internal energy equals heat added to the system minus work done by the system
  • D) the entropy of an isolated system always decreases
C is correct. ΔU = Q − W: the change in internal energy equals heat added to the system minus work done by the system
Q17
Projectile motion is analyzed by
  • A) separating horizontal (constant velocity) and vertical (constant acceleration g downward) components independently
  • B) applying Newton's second law only vertically
  • C) treating the trajectory as a straight line at the launch angle
  • D) using the same equations for horizontal and vertical components
A is correct. separating horizontal (constant velocity) and vertical (constant acceleration g downward) components independently
Q18
Centripetal acceleration is directed
  • A) tangent to the circular path
  • B) outward (centrifugal direction)
  • C) in the direction of velocity
  • D) toward the center of the circular path (inward)
D is correct. toward the center of the circular path (inward)
Q19
The period of a mass-spring system in simple harmonic motion is
  • A) T = 2π × amplitude
  • B) T = 2π√(m/k) — depends on mass and spring constant, not amplitude
  • C) T = 2π√(k/m)
  • D) T = 2π√(g/L)
B is correct. T = 2π√(m/k) — depends on mass and spring constant, not amplitude
Q20
Standing waves form in a medium when
  • A) a single wave reflects from a fixed end with no interference
  • B) the frequency equals the wave speed
  • C) the medium has no resistance to wave propagation
  • D) two waves of equal frequency and amplitude travel in opposite directions and interfere to create nodes and antinodes
D is correct. two waves of equal frequency and amplitude travel in opposite directions and interfere to create nodes and antinodes
