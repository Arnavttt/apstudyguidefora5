Byzantine Art and Its Conventions

Byzantine art (330–1453 CE) is characterized by gold backgrounds representing divine light, stylized flat figures, frontal hieratic poses, and heavy use of mosaic. It prioritizes spiritual meaning over naturalism. Icons are devotional images believed to be conduits to the holy.

Common misconception: Students assume Byzantine flatness indicates artistic inability; it represents a theological choice — earthly naturalism was inappropriate for depicting the divine.

How it can be tested: Identify Byzantine formal conventions and their theological meaning; compare Byzantine and Renaissance treatments of the same subjects.

Key Vocabulary
  • icon — A devotional image of a holy figure in the Eastern Christian tradition, believed to be a window to the divine
  • hieratic — A style that places more important figures higher and larger, conveying spiritual hierarchy
  • mosaic — Art made from small pieces of colored glass, stone, or ceramic (tesserae) set in mortar
  • tympanum — The semi-circular relief panel above a medieval church doorway, typically depicting the Last Judgment
  • sfumato — Leonardo da Vinci's technique of blending tones so smoothly that forms seem to emerge from shadow
Objectives
  • Identify formal features of Byzantine art and their theological meaning
  • Analyze a Renaissance painting for use of perspective and classical references
  • Explain how Gothic cathedrals embodied medieval theology
How this can be tested
  • Compare a Byzantine icon to a Renaissance Madonna
  • Identify flying buttresses and their function in a Gothic cathedral diagram

Q1
The development of linear perspective in early 15th-century Florence is attributed to
  • A) Leonardo da Vinci
  • B) Michelangelo
  • C) Raphael
  • D) Filippo Brunelleschi, whose mathematical demonstration inspired painters like Alberti and Masaccio
D is correct. Filippo Brunelleschi, whose mathematical demonstration inspired painters like Alberti and Masaccio
Q2
The Sistine Chapel ceiling (Michelangelo, 1508–1512) depicts
  • A) scenes from Dante's Divine Comedy
  • B) the history of the Catholic Church
  • C) scenes from Genesis, including the Creation of Adam, surrounded by figures of prophets and sibyls
  • D) the life of Christ from birth to resurrection
C is correct. scenes from Genesis, including the Creation of Adam, surrounded by figures of prophets and sibyls
Q3
Gothic cathedrals used flying buttresses to
  • A) transfer the lateral thrust of the stone vault to external piers, allowing thinner walls and larger windows
  • B) support the tower spires from collapsing
  • C) provide seating for pilgrims outside
  • D) create underground crypts beneath the nave
A is correct. transfer the lateral thrust of the stone vault to external piers, allowing thinner walls and larger windows
Q4
The Baroque style (17th century) was promoted by the Catholic Church to
  • A) imitate the style of classical Greek temples
  • B) reduce decoration and emphasize simplicity
  • C) promote Islamic geometric patterns
  • D) create emotionally powerful art that would re-engage believers and counter Protestant criticism of Catholic imagery
D is correct. create emotionally powerful art that would re-engage believers and counter Protestant criticism of Catholic imagery
Q5
The Virgin of Guadalupe (Mexico, 1531) is significant in colonial Latin American art because it
  • A) was used exclusively by Spanish colonizers
  • B) represents the fusion of indigenous and Spanish Catholic traditions, becoming the most important religious image in Mexico
  • C) is the oldest surviving painting in the Americas
  • D) was created entirely by indigenous artists without European influence
B is correct. represents the fusion of indigenous and Spanish Catholic traditions, becoming the most important religious image in Mexico

Q1
The Book of Kells (Ireland, c. 800 CE) represents Insular art through
  • A) scenes of Viking mythology
  • B) maps of the known medieval world
  • C) intricate interlace patterns, zoomorphic (animal-form) letters, and elaborate illumination serving Christian devotion
  • D) realistic portrait miniatures of Irish saints
C is correct. intricate interlace patterns, zoomorphic (animal-form) letters, and elaborate illumination serving Christian devotion
Q2
Romanesque architecture (c. 1000–1150 CE) is identified by
  • A) large glass windows flooding interiors with light
  • B) classical columns and pediments
  • C) round arches, thick walls with small windows, barrel vaults, and massive piers
  • D) pointed arches and flying buttresses (those are Gothic)
C is correct. round arches, thick walls with small windows, barrel vaults, and massive piers
Q3
The concept of the "tympanum" in a Romanesque or Gothic church refers to
  • A) the decorative floor pattern in the crossing
  • B) the stained glass window above the altar
  • C) the semi-circular carved relief above the main portal, typically showing the Last Judgment
  • D) the bell tower attached to the main nave
C is correct. the semi-circular carved relief above the main portal, typically showing the Last Judgment
Q4
The gold backgrounds in Byzantine mosaics (e.g., Ravenna) represent
  • A) the wealth of the Byzantine emperor who commissioned them
  • B) the landscape of the Holy Land
  • C) the practical advantage of gold reflecting candlelight
  • D) divine, heavenly, eternal light — a non-earthly space signifying the presence of God
D is correct. divine, heavenly, eternal light — a non-earthly space signifying the presence of God
Q5
Leonardo da Vinci's "sfumato" technique creates
  • A) gold leaf highlights on sacred figures
  • B) soft, hazy transitions between light and shadow, giving forms a three-dimensional, atmospheric quality
  • C) hard, sharp outlines emphasizing the human figure
  • D) heavy impasto textures on the painted surface
B is correct. soft, hazy transitions between light and shadow, giving forms a three-dimensional, atmospheric quality
Q6
High Renaissance painting (c. 1490–1527) is characterized by
  • A) flat gold backgrounds and hieratic style (Byzantine)
  • B) idealized forms, balanced composition, serene harmony, and reference to classical antiquity
  • C) emotional intensity and distorted proportions (Mannerism)
  • D) dramatic light/dark contrast (Baroque)
B is correct. idealized forms, balanced composition, serene harmony, and reference to classical antiquity
Q7
Mannerism (c. 1520–1600) in Italian painting is identified by
  • A) strict adherence to classical proportion and harmony
  • B) heavy impasto and broad gestural brushwork
  • C) illusionistic perspective ceilings (that's Baroque)
  • D) elongated figures, unnaturally bright colors, complex poses, and ambiguous spatial logic
D is correct. elongated figures, unnaturally bright colors, complex poses, and ambiguous spatial logic
Q8
El Greco's elongated figures and intense spiritual atmosphere are characteristic of
  • A) Spanish Baroque after Caravaggio
  • B) Mannerism and his personal synthesis of Byzantine iconographic tradition with Italian Renaissance training
  • C) Dutch Golden Age realism
  • D) French Rococo style
B is correct. Mannerism and his personal synthesis of Byzantine iconographic tradition with Italian Renaissance training
Q9
The Baroque sculptor Gianlorenzo Bernini is known for
  • A) serene idealized classical figures in white marble
  • B) politically satirical portraits of Roman senators
  • C) abstract geometric relief panels
  • D) dynamic sculptures that capture movement, dramatic emotion, and theatrical integration of architecture and sculpture (e.g., Ecstasy of Saint Teresa)
D is correct. dynamic sculptures that capture movement, dramatic emotion, and theatrical integration of architecture and sculpture (e.g., Ecstasy of Saint Teresa)
Q10
The syncretism of colonial Latin American art refers to
  • A) importing European artworks unchanged to the Americas
  • B) the blending of indigenous and European artistic traditions in a single work or tradition, as in Andean-Catholic devotional images
  • C) exact copying of Spanish paintings by indigenous artists
  • D) rejection of all indigenous traditions by colonized peoples
B is correct. the blending of indigenous and European artistic traditions in a single work or tradition, as in Andean-Catholic devotional images
