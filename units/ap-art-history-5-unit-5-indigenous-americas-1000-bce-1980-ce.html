Maya Art and Architecture

Maya civilization (c. 250–900 CE Classic period) produced monumental architecture (Tikal), hieroglyphic inscriptions, elaborate polychrome ceramics, and jade carving. The pyramid at Chichén Itzá (El Castillo) functioned as a calendar monument — 91 steps × 4 sides + 1 top = 365, aligning with the solar year.

Common misconception: Students assume all Mesoamerican pyramids are tombs like Egyptian pyramids; most Mesoamerican pyramids are temple platforms for ceremonies, not tombs.

How it can be tested: Analyze formal features of a Maya stela or ceramic; explain cosmological symbolism; compare Mesoamerican and Andean traditions.

Key Vocabulary
  • stela (pl. stelae) — An upright stone slab carved with images and hieroglyphic text, used by Maya rulers to record historical and astronomical events
  • tzompantli — An Aztec skull rack displaying sacrificial victims' skulls at temple complexes
  • quipu — An Incan recording device of knotted strings used for record-keeping and communication
  • featherwork — A prestige material art form in Mesoamerica using iridescent feathers of tropical birds to create elaborate mosaics, cloaks, and headdresses
  • colossal heads — Monumental Olmec basalt heads (c. 900–400 BCE) depicting rulers; up to 9 feet tall

The Olmec colossal heads (c. 900–400 BCE) are believed to represent
  • A) individual Olmec rulers, serving as portraits of political authority
  • B) Olmec gods in human form
  • C) athletes who won the Mesoamerican ballgame
  • D) ancestor spirits watching over the city
A is correct. individual Olmec rulers, serving as portraits of political authority
Q2
Mesoamerican pyramids differ functionally from Egyptian pyramids because Mesoamerican pyramids were primarily
  • A) granaries storing agricultural surplus
  • B) ceremonial temple platforms for ritual activities at their summit, not tombs
  • C) tombs for rulers buried inside them
  • D) observatories for astronomical research
B is correct. ceremonial temple platforms for ritual activities at their summit, not tombs
Q3
The Aztec feathered headdress (Penacho, c. 1500 CE) communicates
  • A) military rank based on the number of feathers
  • B) the wearer's clan membership
  • C) a decorative object with no symbolic significance
  • D) the wearer's elite status and connection to the divine through the use of rare, spiritually significant quetzal bird feathers
D is correct. the wearer's elite status and connection to the divine through the use of rare, spiritually significant quetzal bird feathers
Q4
The Incan city of Machu Picchu (c. 1450 CE) demonstrates Andean architectural skill through
  • A) its bronze reinforcing rods holding stones together
  • B) its exact copy of Spanish colonial architecture
  • C) its use of fired brick throughout
  • D) its dry-stone masonry (ashlar) that uses precisely fitting interlocking stones without mortar
D is correct. its dry-stone masonry (ashlar) that uses precisely fitting interlocking stones without mortar
Q5
Mississippian mound cultures of North America (c. 800–1600 CE) constructed
  • A) large earthen platform mounds used as temple bases, burial sites, and chiefly residences at sites like Cahokia
  • B) stone pyramids similar to Maya structures
  • C) circular stone monuments like Stonehenge
  • D) underground kiva chambers for ceremony
A is correct. large earthen platform mounds used as temple bases, burial sites, and chiefly residences at sites like Cahokia
