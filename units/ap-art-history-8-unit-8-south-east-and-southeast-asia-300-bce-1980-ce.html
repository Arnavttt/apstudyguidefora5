Buddhist Art: Stupa and Sculpture

The Great Stupa at Sanchi (India, c. 300 BCE–100 CE) is a hemispherical reliquary mound with four carved gateways (toranas) depicting jataka tales. Early Buddhist art was aniconic — the Buddha's presence indicated by symbols (footprints, wheel, empty throne). The Gandharan style (1st–5th century CE) introduced anthropomorphic Buddha imagery through contact with Hellenistic traditions along the Silk Road.

Common misconception: Students assume the Buddha was always shown as a human figure; for centuries early Buddhist art used aniconic symbols only. Gandhara introduced figural representation under Greek influence.

How it can be tested: Identify stupa components; explain the shift from aniconic to iconic Buddha representation; compare Gandharan and Mathura Buddha types.

Hindu Temple Architecture

The Hindu temple (mandir) houses a deity in its innermost sanctum (garbhagriha, "womb-chamber"). The Kandariya Mahadeva Temple (Khajuraho, India, c. 1025–1050 CE) has a curving tower (shikhara) representing Mount Meru. The erotic sculpture at Khajuraho reflects tantric theology — physical union symbolizing spiritual union with the divine.

Common misconception: Students are confused by erotic sculpture on Hindu temples; this imagery reflects tantric theology, not moral decadence.

How it can be tested: Identify key components of a Hindu temple; explain the symbolic meaning of the shikhara; analyze the sculptural program at Khajuraho.

Key Vocabulary
  • stupa — A hemispherical Buddhist reliquary mound symbolizing the cosmos and enlightened mind
  • torana — A carved gateway at a stupa depicting stories from the Buddha's lives
  • garbhagriha — The innermost sanctuary of a Hindu temple — the "womb-chamber" housing the deity's image (murti)
  • shikhara — The curving tower of a North Indian Hindu temple, representing Mount Meru
  • Angkor Wat — A 12th-century temple complex in Cambodia dedicated to Vishnu; the world's largest religious monument
  • darshan — In Hindu worship, the auspicious sight of a deity — the worshipper both sees and is seen by the god's image

Early Buddhist art avoided depicting the Buddha directly by using
  • A) using a written description instead of imagery
  • B) depicting the Buddha as a ray of light
  • C) showing only the Buddha's shadow on the ground
  • D) aniconic symbols — the wheel (dharma), Bodhi tree, footprints, and empty throne
D is correct. aniconic symbols — the wheel (dharma), Bodhi tree, footprints, and empty throne
Q2
The stupa's hemispherical form symbolizes
  • A) the cosmos — the dome represents the heavens, the finial the world axis, and circumambulation is an act of worship
  • B) a secular burial mound with no symbolic meaning
  • C) the shape of the Buddha's shaved head
  • D) a military defensive structure
A is correct. the cosmos — the dome represents the heavens, the finial the world axis, and circumambulation is an act of worship
Q3
The erotic sculptures at Khajuraho are interpreted as
  • A) evidence of moral decadence in medieval Hindu society
  • B) scenes from the private life of the temple's patron
  • C) purely decorative imagery with no theological meaning
  • D) expressing tantric theology — physical and spiritual union representing cosmic abundance and the creative force
D is correct. expressing tantric theology — physical and spiritual union representing cosmic abundance and the creative force
Q4
Angkor Wat (Cambodia, 1113–1150 CE) is significant as
  • A) the world's largest religious monument — a Hindu temple complex dedicated to Vishnu that later became Buddhist
  • B) the largest Buddhist monastery ever built
  • C) a Khmer military fortress converted to a temple
  • D) a replica of an Indian Hindu temple built by Indian architects
A is correct. the world's largest religious monument — a Hindu temple complex dedicated to Vishnu that later became Buddhist
Q5
The Gandharan Buddha shows Greek influence through
  • A) the use of bronze rather than stone
  • B) realistic facial features, wavy hair, and toga-like robe — a fusion of Hellenistic naturalism with Buddhist iconography
  • C) depicting the Buddha seated in the lotus position
  • D) the use of the wheel as a dharma symbol
B is correct. realistic facial features, wavy hair, and toga-like robe — a fusion of Hellenistic naturalism with Buddhist iconography
