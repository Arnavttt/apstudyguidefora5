Definition of the Derivative

Learn on this site
Limit Definition

The derivative f'(a) = lim_{h→0} [f(a+h)−f(a)]/h measures the instantaneous rate of change at x=a.

Common misconception: Students confuse the average rate of change (slope of secant) with the derivative (slope of tangent); the limit converts secant to tangent.

How it can be tested: Use the limit definition to find f'(x) for quadratic and simple rational functions.

Differentiability and Continuity

Differentiability implies continuity, but continuity does not imply differentiability. Corners, cusps, and vertical tangents make a function non-differentiable.

Common misconception: Students think any continuous function is differentiable; |x| is continuous but not differentiable at x=0.

How it can be tested: Identify points of non-differentiability from a graph and explain the reason.

Tangent Line Equation

The tangent line at (a, f(a)) has equation y−f(a)=f'(a)(x−a). This is one of the most-tested calculus applications.

Common misconception: Students use the slope of the secant or forget to evaluate the derivative at the specific point.

How it can be tested: Write the equation of the tangent line given a function and x-value.

Key Vocabulary
  • difference quotient — [f(a+h)−f(a)]/h; the slope of a secant line
  • derivative — The limit of the difference quotient as h→0; instantaneous rate of change
  • differentiable — A function with a well-defined derivative at every point in its domain
  • tangent line — The line touching the curve at one point with slope equal to the derivative
Objectives
  • Compute derivatives using the limit definition
  • Determine differentiability from a graph or formula
  • Write tangent line equations at given points
How this can be tested
  • Limit definition may appear in FR to derive a specific derivative
  • Tangent line equations are among the most common FR topics
  • Graphs tested: identify where f is not differentiable
Practice before moving on
  • Use limit definition to find derivative of f(x)=x²+3x
  • Write tangent line to f(x)=x³ at x=2
Key Formulas
  • Limit definition: \( f'(x)=\lim_{h\to 0}\dfrac{f(x+h)-f(x)}{h} \)
  • Tangent line: \( y-f(a)=f'(a)(x-a) \)

Basic Differentiation Rules

Learn on this site
Power and Constant Rules

d/dx[xⁿ]=nxⁿ⁻¹; d/dx[c]=0. These are the foundation of polynomial differentiation.

Common misconception: Students forget to reduce the exponent by 1 or write nxⁿ without adjusting the power.

How it can be tested: Differentiate polynomial and rational functions using the power rule, including negative and fractional exponents.

Product and Quotient Rules

Product: (fg)'=f'g+fg'. Quotient: (f/g)'=(f'g−fg')/g². Both require identifying which part is f and which is g.

Common misconception: Students subtract in the product rule or forget to square the denominator in the quotient rule.

How it can be tested: Differentiate products and quotients of polynomials and trig functions correctly.

Derivatives of Trig Functions

d/dx[sin x]=cos x, d/dx[cos x]=−sin x, d/dx[tan x]=sec²x. The others follow from quotient rule.

Common misconception: Students confuse the derivative of cos x as +sin x instead of −sin x.

How it can be tested: Differentiate expressions combining polynomial and trig functions.

Key Vocabulary
  • power rule — d/dx[xⁿ] = nxⁿ⁻¹
  • product rule — d/dx[f·g] = f'g + fg'
  • quotient rule — d/dx[f/g] = (f'g − fg')/g²
  • derivative of sin/cos — d/dx[sin x]=cos x and d/dx[cos x]=−sin x
Objectives
  • Apply power rule to polynomial, radical, and rational functions
  • Use product and quotient rules correctly
  • Differentiate standard trig functions
How this can be tested
  • Power rule questions appear in almost every exam
  • Product/quotient rule in FR requiring full differentiation
  • Trig derivatives tested in combination with other rules
Practice before moving on
  • Differentiate f(x)=3x⁴−2x⁻¹+√x
  • Find f'(x) for f(x)=x²·sin(x)
Key Formulas
  • Power rule: \( \dfrac{d}{dx}[x^n]=nx^{n-1} \)
  • Product rule: \( (fg)'=f'g+fg' \)
  • Quotient rule: \( \left(\dfrac{f}{g}\right)'=\dfrac{f'g-fg'}{g^2} \)

Chain Rule and Implicit Differentiation

Learn on this site
Chain Rule

If y=f(g(x)), then dy/dx=f'(g(x))·g'(x). Identify the outer and inner function, differentiate from outside in.

Common misconception: Students differentiate only the outer function and forget to multiply by the derivative of the inner function.

How it can be tested: Identify inner and outer functions, then apply chain rule to composite expressions.

Implicit Differentiation

For equations relating x and y where y is not isolated, differentiate both sides with respect to x, applying chain rule to y terms (writing dy/dx), then solve for dy/dx.

Common misconception: Students forget to write dy/dx when differentiating y terms; every y term needs a chain rule factor.

How it can be tested: Use implicit differentiation to find dy/dx for circles, ellipses, and other implicit curves.

Derivatives of Inverse Functions

If f and g are inverses, then g'(x)=1/f'(g(x)). Used to derive d/dx[arcsin x]=1/√(1−x²) and d/dx[arctan x]=1/(1+x²).

Common misconception: Students try to find the inverse function explicitly instead of using the inverse function derivative theorem.

How it can be tested: Apply d/dx[arctan x] and d/dx[arcsin x] in chain rule contexts.

Key Vocabulary
  • chain rule — d/dx[f(g(x))]=f'(g(x))·g'(x)
  • implicit differentiation — Differentiating both sides of an equation with respect to x, treating y as a function of x
  • related rates — Using implicit differentiation with respect to time to relate rates of changing quantities
  • inverse trig derivatives — d/dx[arcsin x]=1/√(1−x²); d/dx[arctan x]=1/(1+x²)
Objectives
  • Apply chain rule to compositions of functions
  • Use implicit differentiation to find dy/dx on implicit curves
  • Differentiate inverse trig functions using the formula
How this can be tested
  • Chain rule appears in almost every exam; often combined with product/quotient rule
  • Implicit differentiation in FR for tangent lines to curves
  • Inverse trig derivatives in MC and FR
Practice before moving on
  • Differentiate y=sin(3x²) and y=(x²+1)⁵
  • Find dy/dx for x²+y²=25 using implicit differentiation
Key Formulas
  • Chain rule: \( \dfrac{dy}{dx}=f'(g(x))\cdot g'(x) \)
  • Implicit diff.: \( \text{Differentiate both sides w.r.t.}\;x;\text{ treat }y\text{ as }y(x) \)

Derivatives of Exponential and Logarithmic Functions

Learn on this site
Derivatives of eˣ and ln(x)

d/dx[eˣ]=eˣ; d/dx[ln x]=1/x. Combined with chain rule: d/dx[e^{u}]=eᵘ·u' and d/dx[ln u]=u'/u.

Common misconception: Students write d/dx[e^{f(x)}]=e^{f(x)} without the chain rule factor f'(x).

How it can be tested: Differentiate composite exponential and logarithmic expressions using chain rule.

Derivatives of aˣ and log_a(x)

d/dx[aˣ]=aˣ·ln(a); d/dx[log_a x]=1/(x·ln a). Base-a functions appear less often than base-e but are tested.

Common misconception: Students forget the ln(a) factor when differentiating aˣ; only eˣ is its own derivative.

How it can be tested: Differentiate base-a exponential and logarithmic functions using the formula.

Logarithmic Differentiation

Take ln of both sides, differentiate implicitly, then solve for y'. Useful for functions like y=x^x or complicated products and quotients.

Common misconception: Students try to apply product/power rules to y=x^x; logarithmic differentiation is required when both base and exponent contain x.

How it can be tested: Use logarithmic differentiation to find the derivative of y=x^x and y=x^{sin x}.

Key Vocabulary
  • d/dx[eˣ] — eˣ (unchanged)
  • d/dx[ln x] — 1/x
  • d/dx[aˣ] — aˣ·ln(a)
  • logarithmic differentiation — Taking ln of both sides and differentiating implicitly to handle variable exponents
Objectives
  • Differentiate exponential and log functions with chain rule
  • Apply base-a derivative formulas
  • Use logarithmic differentiation for variable-exponent functions
How this can be tested
  • e^x derivatives extremely common in FR and MC
  • Logarithmic differentiation tested in BC and advanced AB questions
  • Chain rule always needed for composite exponential/log
Practice before moving on
  • Differentiate f(x)=e^{sin x} and g(x)=ln(x²+1)
  • Use logarithmic differentiation to find d/dx[x^x]
Key Formulas
  • Exponential: \( \dfrac{d}{dx}[e^x]=e^x \)
  • Natural log: \( \dfrac{d}{dx}[\ln x]=\dfrac{1}{x} \)
  • General base: \( \dfrac{d}{dx}[a^x]=a^x\ln a \)
  • Log rule: \( \dfrac{d}{dx}[\log_a x]=\dfrac{1}{x\ln a} \)
