Augmented Sixth Chords

Learn on this site
Italian, French, and German 6th

All three augmented sixth chord types share ♭6̂ and ♯4̂ in the outer voices, creating the augmented sixth interval that expands to an octave (to 5̂) on the dominant. Italian 6th: ♭6̂, 1̂, ♯4̂. French adds 2̂. German adds ♭3̂ (often written as ♯2̂ to avoid parallel fifths).

Common misconception: Students confuse the three types; remember Ger+6 = It+6 + ♭3̂, and Fr+6 = It+6 + 2̂.

How it can be tested: Label augmented sixth chords by type in a score; build each type above a given bass pitch.

Resolution of Aug6 Chords

The augmented sixth interval (♭6̂ to ♯4̂) expands outward by half step to the octave on 5̂ (both notes move to the dominant). The German +6 typically resolves via a cadential 6/4 to avoid parallel fifths with the bass.

Common misconception: Students resolve the ♯4̂ downward; it must move upward by half step to 5̂, while ♭6̂ moves downward by half step.

How it can be tested: Resolve any augmented sixth chord correctly in SATB; identify the resolution in a score.

Key Vocabulary
  • augmented sixth chord — A chromatic predominant chord containing the interval of an augmented sixth between ♭6̂ and ♯4̂, which resolves to the dominant
  • Italian +6 — Contains ♭6̂, 1̂, and ♯4̂
  • French +6 — Contains ♭6̂, 1̂, 2̂, and ♯4̂
  • German +6 — Contains ♭6̂, 1̂, ♭3̂, and ♯4̂; resolves to V or V via I6/4
Objectives
  • Build and identify all three augmented sixth chord types
  • Resolve Aug6 chords to V
  • Explain why the German +6 needs a cadential 6/4
How this can be tested
  • Write an Italian +6 in D minor and resolve it
  • Identify the type of Aug6 in a Bach passage
Practice before moving on
  • Write the German +6 in A minor and show its resolution to I6/4–V
