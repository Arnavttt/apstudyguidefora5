Phrase Structure and Musical Form

Learn on this site
Period and Sentence

A period consists of two phrases (antecedent + consequent): the antecedent ends on a half cadence, the consequent ends on an authentic cadence. A sentence has a basic idea, its repetition/variation, and a continuation fragmenting to a cadence.

Common misconception: Students label any two phrases as a period; a true period requires the antecedent to end on HC and the consequent on PAC or IAC.

How it can be tested: Identify whether a passage is a period or sentence; label antecedent and consequent phrases.

Binary, Ternary, and Rondo Forms

Binary (AB or A:||:BA): two sections, often both repeated. Ternary (ABA): three sections, return of A after contrasting B. Rondo (ABACADA): theme A returns between contrasting episodes. Sonata form: exposition (I → V), development (fragmentation, modulation), recapitulation (returns to I).

Common misconception: Students call any three-part form a "ternary"; sonata form has exposition/development/recapitulation, which is different from simple ABA.

How it can be tested: Label the formal sections of a short piece; identify cadence types at section boundaries.

Key Vocabulary
  • period — A musical structure of two related phrases: antecedent (ending on HC) + consequent (ending on authentic cadence)
  • antecedent — First phrase of a period; ends on a half cadence or weak authentic cadence, creating a question-like feeling
  • consequent — Second phrase of a period; ends on a perfect authentic cadence, providing closure
  • binary form — Two-section form (AB); often both sections repeat
  • ternary form — Three-section form (ABA or ABA'); contrasting middle section between two outer sections

  • Distinguish binary from ternary form

Q1
In a musical period, the antecedent phrase ends with
  • A) a plagal cadence
  • B) a half cadence (HC), creating an open, questioning feeling
  • C) a perfect authentic cadence
  • D) a deceptive cadence
B is correct. a half cadence (HC), creating an open, questioning feeling
Q2
Sonata form consists of three main sections:
  • A) Introduction, Theme, and Coda
  • B) A, B, and A sections
  • C) Prelude, Fugue, and Postlude
  • D) Exposition, Development, and Recapitulation
D is correct. Exposition, Development, and Recapitulation
Q3
In a sentence phrase structure, the continuation section
  • A) begins with a modulation
  • B) fragments the basic idea and drives toward a cadence
  • C) repeats the basic idea exactly
  • D) introduces completely new melodic material
B is correct. fragments the basic idea and drives toward a cadence
Q4
Rounded binary form (ABa) differs from simple binary because
  • A) the A section returns at the end of the B section (in abbreviated or full form)
  • B) both sections are exactly the same length
  • C) the B section never modulates
  • D) the repeat signs are written differently
A is correct. the A section returns at the end of the B section (in abbreviated or full form)
Q5
The development section of sonata form is characterized by
  • A) exact restatement of the exposition
  • B) fragmentation, sequence, and modulation through multiple keys
  • C) stable tonic harmony throughout
  • D) introduction of new main themes
B is correct. fragmentation, sequence, and modulation through multiple keys
