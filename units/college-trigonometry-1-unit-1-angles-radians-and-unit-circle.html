Angle Measurement and the Unit Circle

Learn on this site
Degrees, Radians, and Angle Classification

Angles are measured in degrees (360° = full rotation) or radians (2π rad = full rotation). Conversion: rad = deg × π/180; deg = rad × 180/π. Coterminal angles share the same terminal side, differing by multiples of 2π (or 360°). A reference angle is the acute angle between the terminal side and the x-axis.

Common misconception: Students think radians are "harder" — they are simply a different unit where the angle equals the arc length on a unit circle; for calculus, radians are required.

How it can be tested: Convert between degrees and radians; find coterminal angles; identify reference angles for angles in all quadrants.

The Unit Circle and Exact Trig Values

The unit circle (radius 1, centered at origin) defines trig functions: cos θ = x-coordinate; sin θ = y-coordinate; tan θ = y/x. Key exact values: sin(π/6)=1/2, sin(π/4)=√2/2, sin(π/3)=√3/2, sin(π/2)=1. Signs by quadrant: Q1 all positive; Q2 sin+; Q3 tan+; Q4 cos+ (ASTC: "All Students Take Calculus").

Common misconception: Students memorize values without understanding them — if you know the 30-60-90 and 45-45-90 special triangles, you can derive all unit circle values.

How it can be tested: State exact trig values at special angles; determine signs of trig functions by quadrant; use the ASTC mnemonic.

Key Vocabulary
  • radian — The angle subtended at the center of a circle by an arc equal in length to the radius; 2π radians = 360°
  • coterminal angles — Angles with the same terminal side; they differ by integer multiples of 2π (360°)
  • reference angle — The positive acute angle between the terminal side of an angle and the x-axis; always between 0° and 90°
  • unit circle — A circle of radius 1 centered at the origin; used to define trig functions: (cos θ, sin θ)
  • ASTC — Mnemonic for signs of trig functions by quadrant: All (Q1), Sine (Q2), Tangent (Q3), Cosine (Q4) are positive
