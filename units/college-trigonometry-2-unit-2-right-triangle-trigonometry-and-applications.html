Right Triangles, Law of Sines, and Law of Cosines

Learn on this site
Right Triangle Trigonometry

For acute angle θ in a right triangle: sin θ = opp/hyp, cos θ = adj/hyp, tan θ = opp/adj (SOH-CAH-TOA). Inverse trig functions find angles: θ = arctan(opp/adj). Angles of elevation (looking up from horizontal) and depression (looking down from horizontal) use the same trig ratios with a horizontal reference.

Common misconception: Students confuse "angle of elevation" and "angle of depression" — both are measured from the horizontal, not from the vertical.

How it can be tested: Apply SOH-CAH-TOA to find missing sides and angles; solve right triangle applications including angles of elevation and depression.

Law of Sines and Law of Cosines

Law of Sines: a/sin A = b/sin B = c/sin C — use when you know AAS, ASA, or SSA. Law of Cosines: c^2 = a^2 + b^2 - 2ab cos C — use when you know SAS or SSS. The ambiguous case (SSA) can produce 0, 1, or 2 triangles depending on whether the given side is long enough to reach the base. Area formula: K = (1/2)ab sin C.

Common misconception: Students always try to use the Law of Sines for every triangle — use the Law of Cosines when no angle-side pair is given (SSS or SAS).

How it can be tested: Select and apply Law of Sines or Cosines; handle the ambiguous SSA case; calculate triangle area using the sine area formula.

Key Vocabulary
  • SOH-CAH-TOA — Mnemonic for right triangle trig ratios: Sin=Opp/Hyp, Cos=Adj/Hyp, Tan=Opp/Adj
  • angle of elevation — The angle measured upward from the horizontal to the line of sight
  • angle of depression — The angle measured downward from the horizontal to the line of sight
  • Law of Sines — a/sin A = b/sin B = c/sin C; used for AAS, ASA, or SSA triangle cases
  • Law of Cosines — c^2 = a^2 + b^2 - 2ab cos C; used for SAS or SSS triangle cases
  • ambiguous case — The SSA situation where two triangles, one triangle, or no triangle may satisfy the given conditions
Objectives
  • Apply SOH-CAH-TOA to solve right triangles
  • Select the appropriate law (Sines or Cosines) for a given triangle
  • Solve the ambiguous SSA case
How this can be tested
  • A ladder 10 ft long leans against a wall, making a 65° angle with the ground. Find the height it reaches.
  • In triangle ABC, a=8, b=5, C=60°. Find side c using the Law of Cosines.
Practice before moving on
  • How do you know whether SSA gives 0, 1, or 2 triangles?
Key Formulas
  • SOH-CAH-TOA: \( \sin\theta=\dfrac{opp}{hyp},\quad\cos\theta=\dfrac{adj}{hyp},\quad\tan\theta=\dfrac{opp}{adj} \)
  • Law of Sines: \( \dfrac{a}{\sin A}=\dfrac{b}{\sin B}=\dfrac{c}{\sin C} \)
  • Law of Cosines: \( c^2=a^2+b^2-2ab\cos C \)

In a right triangle, if the opposite side = 5 and hypotenuse = 13, then sin θ equals
  • A) 5/12
  • B) 12/13
  • C) 5/13 — sin = opposite/hypotenuse
  • D) 13/5

Q2
The Law of Cosines is used when you know
  • A) SSA only (ambiguous case)
  • B) only right triangles
  • C) SAS (two sides and the included angle) or SSS (all three sides)
  • D) AAS or ASA (two angles and a side)
C is correct. SAS (two sides and the included angle) or SSS (all three sides)
Q3
An angle of depression from a cliff to a boat is 25°. This angle is measured
  • A) downward from the vertical (straight down)
  • B) upward from the horizon at the boat
  • C) from the base of the cliff to the boat
  • D) downward from the horizontal at the observer's eye level
D is correct. downward from the horizontal at the observer's eye level
Q4
The Law of Sines fails to uniquely determine a triangle in the
  • A) AAS case
  • B) SSA (side-side-angle) case — this is the ambiguous case where 0, 1, or 2 triangles may exist
  • C) ASA case
  • D) SAS case
B is correct. SSA (side-side-angle) case — this is the ambiguous case where 0, 1, or 2 triangles may exist
Q5

  • A) 6√3
