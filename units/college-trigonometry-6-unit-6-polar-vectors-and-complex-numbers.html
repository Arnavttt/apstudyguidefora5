Polar Coordinates and Graphs

Polar coordinates (r, θ) describe a point by distance r from the origin and angle θ. Convert to rectangular: x = r cosθ, y = r sinθ. Convert to polar: r = √(x^2+y^2), θ = arctan(y/x) (adjust quadrant). Polar graphs: r = a (circle), r = 2a cosθ (circle shifted), r = a + b cosθ (limaçon); if a=b: cardioid; r = a sin(nθ) or cos(nθ): rose curve with 2n petals (n even) or n petals (n odd).

Common misconception: Students use θ = arctan(y/x) without checking the quadrant — the arctan formula only gives angles in Q1 or Q4; adjust for Q2 and Q3.

How it can be tested: Convert between polar and rectangular coordinates; graph polar equations; identify limaçons, cardioids, and rose curves.

Vectors and Complex Numbers in Trigonometric Form

A vector v = ⟨a, b⟩ has magnitude |v| = √(a^2+b^2) and direction θ = arctan(b/a). Dot product: v·w = a₁a₂+b₁b₂ = |v||w|cosθ (θ = angle between vectors). A complex number z = a+bi can be written in polar form z = r(cosθ + i sinθ) = re^(iθ), where r=|z| and θ=arg(z). De Moivre's Theorem: z^n = r^n(cos(nθ) + i sin(nθ)). nth roots: n roots evenly spaced by 2π/n.

Common misconception: Students think all vectors can be "divided" like scalars — you can compute dot products but vector division is not defined.

How it can be tested: Compute vector magnitudes and dot products; convert complex numbers to polar form; apply De Moivre's Theorem.

Key Vocabulary
  • polar coordinates — (r, θ) — r is distance from origin; θ is angle from positive x-axis
  • cardioid — A heart-shaped polar curve: r = a(1 + cosθ) or r = a(1 + sinθ); special case of limaçon with a=b
  • rose curve — A polar curve r = a sin(nθ) or r = a cos(nθ); n petals if n is odd, 2n petals if n is even
  • dot product — v·w = a₁a₂+b₁b₂; equals |v||w|cosθ where θ is the angle between vectors; result is a scalar
  • trigonometric form — z = r(cosθ + i sinθ) — polar form of a complex number; r is modulus, θ is argument
  • De Moivre's Theorem — z^n = r^n(cos nθ + i sin nθ); used to find powers and nth roots of complex numbers

  • Identify and graph polar curves (cardioid, rose)

Q1
The rectangular coordinates equivalent to the polar point (2, π/3) are
  • A) (2, √3)
  • B) (√3, 1)
  • C) (1, 2)
  • D) (1, √3) — x = 2cos(π/3) = 2(1/2) = 1; y = 2sin(π/3) = 2(√3/2) = √3
D is correct. (1, √3) — x = 2cos(π/3) = 2(1/2) = 1; y = 2sin(π/3) = 2(√3/2) = √3
Q2
The polar equation r = 3 describes
  • A) a line passing through the origin at angle 3
  • B) a spiral
  • C) a parabola
  • D) a circle of radius 3 centered at the origin
D is correct. a circle of radius 3 centered at the origin
Q3
If vectors v = ⟨3, 4⟩ and w = ⟨0, 1⟩, then v · w equals
  • A) 3
  • B) 7
  • C) ⟨0, 4⟩
  • D) 4 — dot product: 3(0) + 4(1) = 4
D is correct. 4 — dot product: 3(0) + 4(1) = 4
Q4
Two vectors are orthogonal (perpendicular) if their dot product is
  • A) equal to their magnitudes
  • B) negative
  • C) 0 — v·w = |v||w|cos(90°) = |v||w|(0) = 0
  • D) 1
C is correct. 0 — v·w = |v||w|cos(90°) = |v||w|(0) = 0
Q5
The modulus of the complex number 3 + 4i is
  • A) √7
  • B) 5 — |z| = √(3^2+4^2) = √25 = 5
  • C) 7
  • D) 3
B is correct. 5 — |z| = √(3^2+4^2) = √25 = 5
